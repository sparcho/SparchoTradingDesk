PER-COMPANY (condensed)

TCS — largest, cheapest on every multiple (15.6x, ROCE 63%, ~5.2% yield, 5 buybacks ~₹83,000cr) but FY26 revenue −2.4% CC; EBIT 25% (4-yr high); TCV $40.7bn; AI run-rate >$2.3bn. Overhang: 17-Apr-2026 Nashik-unit internal investigation. (TCS Q4FY26 PR)

INFY — #2; FY26 +3.1% CC, OM 20.3%, PAT ~+10%; TCV $14.9bn; ₹18,000cr 2025 buyback + 4.5% yield. Cautious FY27 guide +1.5–3.5% CC. AI >5.5% of rev. (SEC 6-K, 23-Apr)

HCLTECH — IT + strong ER&D (ERS +9.8% CC) + HCLSoftware; FY26 +3.9% CC, EBIT 17.2%, ~91% payout/~4.7% yield (no buyback). PAT hit by Q4 exceptionals. Slowest-ever FY27 guide 1–4%. AI run-rate >$620mn. (HCLTech PR, 21-Apr)

PERSISTENT — fastest grower (+17.4% USD, PAT +33%), ROE 22.2%, at ~46x (≈3x mega-cap multiple — no room for error). 24th straight quarter of USD growth; $2B run-rate by FY27. (Persistent PR, 21-Apr)

KPIT — auto-software/ER&D pure-play; FY26 +1.3% CC, PAT −24% (depreciation/interest up), ~30–33x (below 10-yr median ~47x). Caresoft acq; FY27 P&S guided ~+30%. Chairman Ravi Pandit passed away 08-May-2026. Risk: OEM R&D slowdown, client concentration. (KPIT PR/Autocar)

TATAELXSI — design+media+ER&D; decelerated hard — FY26 rev +0.8% (5-yr CAGR was 16%), PAT −20%, OPM 23% (from 31% peak), attrition 15.8%; ~37–41x vs 3-yr avg ~44x. Q4 recovery; DevStudio.ai. Risk: media/healthcare lumpiness, OEM concentration (77% of transportation). (Tata Elxsi PR)

Benchmarks: WIPRO ~14x, FY26 IT-services +0.3% CC (near-flat), ₹15,000cr buyback. LTIM ~22.6x, Q4 +8.1% YoY. Industry (NASSCOM SR-2026): India tech ~$315bn (+6.1% FY26); 2026–27 framed as "AI investment years."

SYNTHESIS

The whole Indian IT complex has de-rated hard (all six near 52-wk lows, −20 to −43% over 1yr), yet the cohort is sharply bifurcated. The mega-caps are cheap, cash-rich and slow: TCS (15.6x, ROCE 63%, ~5% yield, five buybacks) actually shrank −2.4% CC, while INFY (+3.1%) and HCLTECH (+3.9%) grew low-single-digits and both guide a cautious 1–4% CC for FY27 — their appeal is capital return and balance-sheet quality, not growth. The specialists invert that trade-off: PERSISTENT is the clear growth winner (+17.4% USD, +33% PAT) and priced for it at ~46x — roughly 3x the mega-cap multiple, the starkest expression of the valuation gap, with no room for a stumble. The ER&D pure-plays look more vulnerable despite still-rich multiples: KPIT (+1.3% CC, PAT −24%, ~30–33x) and TATAELXSI (+0.8%, PAT −20%, ~37–41x) both saw growth collapse and earnings fall, with Tata Elxsi's margins compressing 31%→23% over four years. The differentiator splits cleanly: scale/cash/returns (TCS, HCLTECH, INFY) vs growth/niche at a premium (PERSISTENT, and structurally KPIT/TATAELXSI though currently decelerating). AI is the swing factor — TCS's >$2.3bn and HCLTECH's >$620mn run-rates show monetization beginning, but the same AI threatens to cannibalize legacy services.
