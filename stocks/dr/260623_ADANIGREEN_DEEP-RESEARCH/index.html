Executive Summary

Adani Green Energy is India's largest renewable-energy IPP — solar/wind/hybrid plants sold under long-term PPAs. FY26: power-supply revenue ₹11,602 Cr (+22%), core EBITDA ₹10,865 Cr (+23%, ~91% margin), cash profit ₹5,399 Cr (+11%); operational capacity grew 35% to 19.3 GW (from 14.2 GW), with a record 5.1 GW added; Q4 PAT ₹397 Cr (+72.6%) (solarquarter/adani/univest, FY26). Target: 50 GW by 2030. But leverage is very high — ~₹1.5 lakh cr+ debt — and the stock trades at ~92× P/E (mcap ~₹1,48,600 Cr, Apr-26). Promoter (Adani) ~57-60%.

CORE VERDICT. Adani Green is the scale "operator leg" of the renewables chain — India's largest IPP with a 35% capacity ramp, a ~91% EBITDA-margin PPA-annuity model, and a 50 GW-by-2030 ambition; the execution (5.1 GW added FY26) is real. But it is the most-leveraged and among the most-expensive names in the program: ~₹1.5 lakh cr+ debt + ~92× PE. The annuity EBITDA is genuine, but the heavy interest burden + capex-funding needs + Adani-group leverage/governance perception are the dominant risks. The load-bearing question is whether capacity + cash flow grow fast enough to service/de-lever while justifying ~92× PE. Scale + execution are real; debt + valuation are the severe constraints.
