2026-06-23 · status active

One-line scope: comparative positioning of BAJFINANCE · CHOLAFIN · SHRIRAMFIN · PBFINTECH · PAYTM across loan-book/AUM growth, spreads, asset quality, capital, returns and valuation — with P/B as the key lens. Contrasts the diversified consumer-NBFC vs the vehicle-financiers vs the fintech-lenders/marketplaces. No buy/avoid call.

Read first — heterogeneous cohort. These five are NOT like-for-like. BAJFINANCE is a diversified consumer/SME balance-sheet NBFC. CHOLAFIN and SHRIRAMFIN are vehicle-financiers (Chola: new-vehicle + home/SME tilt; Shriram: used-CV / deep-value franchise). PBFINTECH (Policybazaar) is an insurance/credit marketplace — it does NOT carry a lending book, so bank-style asset-quality ratios (GNPA/NNPA/CRAR/NIM) are N/A. PAYTM (One97) is a payments + financial-services distribution platform — its lending is largely off-balance-sheet (DLG/distribution), so the same bank ratios are N/A. Do NOT read the asset-quality rows across the fintech pair. Cross-name P/B is meaningful only for the three lenders; the two fintechs are priced on growth/optionality, not book.

Plain-English Glossary

Lending domain

TermMeaning
AUMAssets Under Management — the loan book (on + managed/off-book).
NIMNet Interest Margin — yield on assets minus cost of funds, as % of assets. Higher = fatter spread.
GNPA / NNPAGross / Net Non-Performing Assets % — bad loans before/after provisions. Lower = cleaner book.
Credit costProvisions/write-offs as % of book — the through-the-cycle cost of lending.
CRARCapital-to-Risk-Weighted-Assets Ratio — regulatory capital cushion (RBI min ~15% for NBFCs).
ROA / ROEReturn on Assets / Equity — profitability per ₹ of assets / shareholder capital.
Cost-to-income (CIR)Operating cost as % of income — efficiency (lower = leaner).
Securitisation / co-lendingSelling/sharing loans to free capital + earn fees (off-book AUM).
DLG / FLDG(First-Loss) Default-Loss Guarantee — a fintech distributor's capped credit risk on loans it originates for a lender's book; the Paytm-style off-B/S model.
Take-rateA marketplace/platform's revenue as % of the GMV/premium it intermediates (the PBFintech/Paytm lens, not a bank ratio).

Valuation terms

TermMeaning
P/B (key)Price-to-Book — market cap ÷ net worth. The primary valuation lens for lenders; a premium P/B must be earned by a high ROE.
P/EPrice-to-Earnings. Secondary lens; the primary lens for the fintech pair (no meaningful book).

Executive Summary

The three lenders are all growing the book double-digit with broadly healthy capital, but they separate sharply on asset quality × returns × price: BAJFINANCE is the best-in-class compounder (GNPA ~1.0%, ROA ~4.5%) priced at a deep P/B premium (5.2×); CHOLAFIN pairs the highest ROE (~23% Q4) with a 4.0× P/B; SHRIRAMFIN is the cheapest (P/B 2.2×) with the fattest NIM (8.6%) but the highest GNPA (4.58%) — a classic deep-value-vs-quality trade. The two fintechs are a different game entirely: PBFINTECH (marketplace) and PAYTM (distribution) both inflected to strong profit growth in FY26 but are priced on platform optionality (PBFintech ~136× trailing P/E), not book.

CORE READ (no buy/avoid). On the P/B-vs-ROE lens: BAJFINANCE — premium P/B (5.2×) earned by best-in-class asset quality + ~19–20% ROE; the quality compounder. CHOLAFIN — 4.0× P/B against the cohort's highest ROE (~23% Q4); premium justified by returns if asset quality holds. SHRIRAMFIN — cheapest (2.2× P/B) on the highest NIM but highest GNPA; the deep-value / higher-risk lender. PBFINTECH — not a P/B story; an asset-light insurance-marketplace compounder priced for years of growth. PAYTM — a freshly-profitable platform turnaround priced on optionality, not book. Each archetype is a different bet; the cohort is not a single screen.

P/B-vs-ROE positioning read (the key lens):

  • BAJFINANCE — 5.2× P/B on ~19–20% ROE + 1.0% GNPA: the premium is the price of best-in-class quality + scale + deposit franchise. Expensive, but the cleanest book.
  • CHOLAFIN — 4.0× P/B on the cohort's highest ROE (~23%): on a pure P/B-÷-ROE basis the most internally consistent premium of the three lenders, contingent on asset quality holding.
  • SHRIRAMFIN — 2.2× P/B on ~19% ROE: the cheapest re-rating candidate IF credit cost stays contained; the GNPA 4.58% is why the market discounts it.
  • PBFINTECH — ~136× P/E is a growth/optionality multiple, not a book multiple; the variant-perception question is whether insurance-distribution + Paisabazaar can sustain the implied growth.
  • PAYTM — priced on the durability of its FY26 turnaround (first profit, GMV +27%, FS-distribution +52%) and net-cash optionality, not on book.

Variant-perception vs consensus (per name, brief):

  • BAJFINANCE: consensus = quality compounder; variant risk = law-of-large-numbers growth deceleration vs the 5.2× P/B.
  • CHOLAFIN: consensus = best-execution vehicle financier; variant risk = the cautious-overlay/GNPA print is an early cycle signal.
  • SHRIRAMFIN: consensus = cheap, re-rating on merger synergies; variant = the discount is earned by used-CV credit risk.
  • PBFINTECH: consensus = asset-light secular winner; variant = 136× P/E leaves no room for a growth wobble.
  • PAYTM: consensus = profitable-platform re-rating; variant = profitability is partly cost-cut-led; revenue durability + regulatory overhang unproven.

Section 7 / Per-name capsules

BAJFINANCE — Thesis: scale + cleanest book + deposit franchise = durable compounder. Bull: AUM compounding 20%+ at 4.5% ROA. Base: growth moderates to high-teens, ROE ~19%. Bear: large-base deceleration de-rates the 5.2× P/B. Catalyst: quarterly AUM + GNPA prints. Kill-switch: GNPA breaks >2% or ROE <17%.

CHOLAFIN — Thesis: best-ROE vehicle financier with optionality in home/SME. Bull: ROA holds 4%+, ROE ~23%, book +20%. Base: overlay normalises, ROE low-20s. Bear: the GNPA uptick is cyclical, not one-off. Catalyst: asset-quality trend post-overlay. Kill-switch: GNPA trend worsens 2 consecutive quarters.

SHRIRAMFIN — Thesis: cheap, high-NIM used-CV franchise re-rating on scale. Bull: GNPA contained, P/B re-rates toward 3×. Base: steady ~19% ROE, 2.2× P/B holds. Bear: CV cycle turns, credit cost spikes. Catalyst: NIM + GNPA stability. Kill-switch: GNPA >5.5% or NIM <8%.

PBFINTECH — Thesis: asset-light insurance-distribution flywheel. Bull: premium +40%/yr, margin to mid-teens. Base: +30% rev, margin ~10%. Bear: 136× P/E de-rates on any growth slowdown. Catalyst: premium-collection growth + new-initiative margins. Kill-switch: core insurance broking growth <25%.

PAYTM — Thesis: profitable payments platform with FS-distribution optionality. Bull: FS-distribution scales, sustained profit, re-rating. Base: steady profit, GMV +20%+. Bear: profitability is cost-led; regulatory/competition caps revenue. Catalyst: sustained PAT + FS-distribution run-rate. Kill-switch: return to operating loss or a regulatory action.
