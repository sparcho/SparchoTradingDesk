One-line: DIXON · KAYNES · SYRMA · AMBER on growth, margins, valuation, returns and working-capital — margins/multiples diverge because the segment mixes diverge. No buy/avoid call.

Mix drives everything: DIXON = mobile/EMS ~90% (thin ~4% EBITDA, high volume); KAYNES = industrial/auto/aero EMS (~16% EBITDA) + new OSAT semiconductor packaging; SYRMA = diversified higher-margin niches (~11% EBITDA); AMBER = RAC/AC contract mfg ~70% (~8%, seasonal) + electronics + railway/defence (Sidwal).
The whole cohort is expensive — the differentiation is what you pay the multiple for.

PER-COMPANY (condensed)

DIXON — India's largest EMS; mobile/EMS ~90%, structurally thin (~4–5% EBITDA) but scale + negative working capital → cohort-best ROCE (~32–42%), CCC −7d, near net-cash. FY26 rev ~₹49,000cr (+26–28%), PAT ₹1,644cr (+33%, incl ₹734cr other income). Vivo JV (pending), HKC display + Gemtek JVs. Risk: Motorola concentration (>45%), mobile PLI expiry, ~4% margins amplify swings. P/E spread 52–99x reflects earnings-quality noise. (Business Standard/scanx/Screener, May 2026)

KAYNES — high-mix EMS (industrial/auto/aero/defence) + the only OSAT semiconductor-packaging play here (Sanand, ₹3,300cr, commercial production reached). FY26 rev ₹3,626cr (+33%), EBITDA ~16%, PAT ₹364cr (+24%); order book ~₹8,400–9,000cr. But: CCC blew out to 193d, FCF −₹1,841cr on the semicon build; ROE 9.6%/ROCE 13.2% (capex depressing returns pre-revenue); ~76–86x P/E after two guidance trims + weak Q4 (−22%). (FY26 concall/Business Standard)

SYRMA — diversified design-led ("ODM") EMS skewed to higher-margin niches; the balanced compounder. FY26 rev ₹4,819cr (+27%), OPM ~11.3% (+270bps), PAT ₹346cr (+87%) — a margin-mix inflection. Order book ~₹6,600cr; net cash ~₹467cr; ROE 18.3%/ROCE 20%. Johari + Elcome acquisitions; Kaga (Japan) pact Jun-2026. Risk: ~80x P/E, working-capital intensity, integration. (Q4FY26 concall)

AMBER — India's largest RAC contract mfr (~26–27% share) + electronics (ILJIN PCBA, +49%) + railway/defence (Sidwal, ~16–18% margin). FY26 rev ₹12,186cr (+22%), blended margin ~8%, adj PAT ₹338cr (+22%). Highly seasonal (Q2/Q3 loss-making). Railway order book ₹2,600+cr. Risk: RAC seasonality/weak summer, ~7% RAC margins, debt (₹2,793cr Sep-25), valuation. Electronics demerger/IPO PENDING (not announced). (company PR/Whalesbook)

Benchmarks: Cyient DLM — aero/defence/medical EMS, FY26 rev −17%, order book ₹2,417cr, ~66x. Avalon — industrial/clean-energy, FY26 rev +46%/PAT +78%, ~104–132x.

SYNTHESIS

On revenue, KAYNES (+33%), SYRMA (+27%) and DIXON (+26–28%) lead, with AMBER (+22%) trailing — but on PAT growth SYRMA (+87%) wins outright, a margin-mix inflection rather than just volume. Margins follow the mix exactly: KAYNES (~16%) > SYRMA (~11%) > AMBER (~8%) > DIXON (~4–5%) — Dixon's scale does not buy margin; mobile EMS is structurally thin. The whole cohort is expensive, but the ranking matters: SYRMA (~80x P/E, ~42x EV/EBITDA) and AMBER (82–190x) are richest; KAYNES (~76–86x) is rich and has already de-rated ~35–44% off its high on two guidance trims; DIXON is "cheapest" on EV/EBITDA (~27.5x) though its 52–99x P/E spread reflects earnings-quality noise. On returns, DIXON leads (ROCE ~32–42% on negative working capital, asset-light scale), SYRMA second (ROCE 20%), while KAYNES (13%) and AMBER (~10–14%) lag — KAYNES because OSAT capex depresses returns before it generates revenue, AMBER because of seasonal, capital-heavy RAC. Balance sheet: DIXON (CCC −7d, near net-cash) and SYRMA (net cash) win; KAYNES is the clear laggard (CCC 193d, FCF −₹1,841cr on the semicon build); AMBER carries the most absolute debt. Net differentiator: DIXON = scale + capital efficiency; KAYNES = margin + the semicon (OSAT) optionality, unproven and cash-hungry; SYRMA = the balanced margin-and-balance-sheet compounder; AMBER = a three-engine RAC+electronics+railway story dragged by AC seasonality. The multiple is uniformly demanding — the question is which engine you're paying for.
