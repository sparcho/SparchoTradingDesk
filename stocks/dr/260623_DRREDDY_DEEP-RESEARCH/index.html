Executive Summary

Dr. Reddy's is a generics-heavy global pharma. FY26: revenue ₹33,593 Cr (+3.2%), consolidated PAT ₹4,285 Cr (−24%); Q4 PAT plunged 86% to ₹220 Cr on one-offs — lenalidomide shelf-stock-adjustment (₹453 Cr), CAR-T/Eftilagimod impairment (₹228 Cr), VAT provision (₹114 Cr) (whalesbook/investywise, FY26). ~24-25% EBITDA; net cash; ₹8/sh dividend. Promoter ~26.7%.

CORE VERDICT. Dr. Reddy's is a cash-rich generics major in a transition year — FY26 revenue grew only +3.2% and PAT fell −24% (Q4 −86%) on the lenalidomide/Revlimid normalisation + one-off charges (SSA, impairments, VAT). It's cheaper than Sun (~23× vs ~38×) but the trade-off is low growth + a generics base lapping its biggest profit driver. The forward pivot — biosimilars, consumer (Nicotinell), GLP-1 opportunity, new markets — must replace Revlimid. The honest framing: a quality, cash-rich franchise navigating a post-Revlimid air-pocket; the re-acceleration (biosimilars/new bets) is the swing, not the current numbers. Cash-rich + cheaper-than-Sun; low growth + Revlimid-cliff + one-offs are the constraints. A value/transition pharma, not a current grower.
