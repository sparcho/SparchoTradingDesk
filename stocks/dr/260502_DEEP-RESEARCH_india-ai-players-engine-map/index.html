EXECUTIVE SUMMARY — CORE FINDING

India's AI stack has bifurcated into two cleanly attributable engine camps, plus a large UNDISCLOSED middle layer.

The two attributable camps:

  1. Sovereign / domestic-engine camp — IndiaAI Mission funded, MeitY-routed: 12 teams (Sarvam, BharatGen, Hanooman, Project Indus / Tech Mahindra, Gnani, Soket, Gan, Avataar, GenLoop, Zenteq, Intellihealth, Shodh, Fractal). Sarvam-105B trained from scratch on 12 trillion tokens at the IndiaAI Impact Summit Feb 2026 is the flagship sovereign claim. Krutrim-2 (Ola) sits adjacent — built on Mistral-NeMo 12B architecture, not from-scratch. Both publish weights. Domestic is the honest tag for the lab itself; Mistral-derivative is the honest tag for the underlying architecture for several "sovereign" models (Sarvam-M, Krutrim-2). Sarvam-105B is the cleanest from-scratch claim.
  1. Hyperscaler-engine camp — Indian IT-services + verticals running on US foundation models. Three sub-patterns: (a) Anthropic-design-partner / Claude-customer — Infosys (Topaz + Claude), Air India (Claude Code), Pratham (Anytime Testing Machine on Claude); (b) OpenAI-strategic — Tata Group (Tata-OpenAI multi-dimensional deal), HCLTech (OpenAI strategic), JioHotstar (OpenAI search), PhonePe; (c) Google Cloud / Gemini — TCS (TCS Generative AI on Google Cloud), Wipro (Lab45 on Google Cloud + multi-LLM), Cropin (Google Cloud backbone). Several are HYBRID — Infosys runs both Claude and OpenAI tools; HCLTech AI Force on AWS Bedrock multi-engine (Claude / OpenAI / Gemini / Llama / Granite); Reliance Jio engages OpenAI + Meta + Google + NVIDIA simultaneously.

The UNDISCLOSED middle layer is large: Tata Elxsi, KPIT, Tanla Platforms, Newgen, Genesys, Info Edge, PB Fintech, Angel One, CDSL, Pristyn, Practo, MAPMYINDIA's Drive GPT (despite the brand suggesting a transformer). For these names, the engine choice is either truly undisclosed or buried inside white-labelled vendor agreements; the operator-actionable read is "do not assume the engine; ask in the next concall or stay UNDISCLOSED-tagged in the watchlist scoring."

PART I — STRUCTURAL MAP

I.1 The 7-archetype taxonomy

Refined from the operator-supplied seven buckets:

  1. Foundation-model labs — entities that train (or claim to train) their own model weights, sovereign or otherwise. Sub-split into (a) from-scratch sovereign, (b) fine-tuned over open-weight base, (c) wrappers on hosted APIs claiming model-status.
  2. AI infrastructure / cloud / DC — engine-agnostic compute providers (GPU, DC capacity, sovereign-AI cloud). Beneficiaries of the engine wars; not engine-bets.
  3. Vertical AI applications — domain-specific products (health, legal, finance, agri, edtech, GIS) that consume engines via API or fine-tuned wrappers.
  4. Enterprise IT-services AI practices — global integrators (TCS, Infosys, Wipro, HCLTech, LTIMindtree, Tech Mahindra) with named AI products (Topaz, AI Force, Lab45, Ignio, Maker's Lab) and partnership deals with multiple model vendors.
  5. AI-native consumer apps — chatbots, generative tools, productivity (Quizizz, Perplexity-India, etc.).
  6. AI hardware / edge — chip startups + edge-AI plays. Less mature in India; Tenstorrent has a Bangalore office, Polymage Labs (IISc-incubated) builds compiler tooling for Tenstorrent. No domestic AI-accelerator pure-play of scale yet.
  7. Listed pure-plays vs unlisted vs subsidiaries-of-conglomerates — orthogonal flag, not its own bucket. Tagged in Part III matrix.

I.3 The IndiaAI Mission as a forcing variable

The IndiaAI Mission (MeitY, March 2024 launch, ₹10,372 crore outlay) is the single biggest structural shaper of which Indian AI labs get to play. Two pillars are load-bearing for this brief:

  • IndiaAI Compute — 38,000+ GPUs (mostly NVIDIA, mostly via Yotta + Jio + others) onboarded to common compute facility, available to startups and academia at subsidised rates (~₹65/hour cited in Indian Masterminds coverage). Means: an Indian lab does not need to own a hyperscaler relationship to train a 100B-parameter model. This is the precondition for Sarvam-105B's "from scratch" claim.
  • Innovation Centre — 12 selected teams (Sarvam, BharatGen / IIT Bombay consortium, Soket AI, Gnani AI, Gan AI, Avataar AI, GenLoop, Zenteq, Intellihealth, Shodh AI, Fractal Analytics, Tech Mahindra Maker's Lab) with funding allocations totaling ~₹2,194 crore (BharatGen ₹1,058.52 crore is the largest single allocation; Sarvam ₹246.72 crore; Tech Mahindra ₹2.66 crore is the smallest — interesting given Project Indus already exists). Source: DD News + The Tribune (secondary).

The IndiaAI Mission operates as a subsidy on the domestic-engine camp specifically. It does NOT subsidise the hyperscaler-engine camp, which is funded by their own balance sheets (Anthropic / OpenAI / Google direct partnerships).

I.4 Anthropic's India footprint as a 2026 inflection

Anthropic opened a Bengaluru office February 2026 (second Asia office after Tokyo), led by Irina Ghose. India is now Anthropic's #2 consumer market by Claude usage, after the US. Run-rate revenue in India has doubled since the October 2025 announcement. Named partnerships at the office launch:

  • Pratham (education NGO) — Anthropic's first strategic India AI lab partner; Anytime Testing Machine on Claude piloted with 1,500 students across 20 schools; 100-school target end-2026.
  • Air India (Tata Group subsidiary) — Claude Code customer for developer tooling.
  • Central Square Foundation — API credits for AI-EdTech tools.
  • EkStep Foundation — agriculture-focused AI applications.
  • Infosys (separate Feb 17 2026 deal) — Topaz integrates Claude + Claude Code; Anthropic Center of Excellence; starting in telecom vertical.
  • Indic-language model effort — company-wide push to strengthen 10 widely spoken Indian languages (Hindi / Bengali / Tamil / Urdu et al). Not a model release; a data + tuning programme.

Source: anthropic.com/news/bengaluru-office-partnerships-across-india + secondary coverage. Tier: PRIMARY.

This matters for engine attribution because it changes the prior on which Indian players are likely to be Claude-adjacent over the next 12 months: anyone building developer tooling (Air-India-pattern), any education NGO with corpus depth (Pratham-pattern), and any IT-services firm building telecom-vertical agents (Infosys-pattern) is now a higher-probability Anthropic prospect than they were six months ago. Operator-relevant: this strengthens the INFOSYS engine-side moat narrative without changing TCS's engine-side narrative (which remains Google + OpenAI via Tata-OpenAI deal).

V.1 — L1 Plain-language summary (5–7 bullets)

  • Indian AI is split into two real camps + a big undisclosed middle. Camp A is the sovereign-domestic camp funded by IndiaAI Mission (Sarvam, BharatGen, Hanooman, Project Indus, etc.); Camp B is the hyperscaler-engine camp (Anthropic / OpenAI / Google customers). The undisclosed middle is most Indian listed mid-caps that publicly say "we use AI" but never say which engine.
  • Anthropic's India footprint stepped up in Feb 2026. Bengaluru office, Pratham + Air India + Infosys + EkStep + CSF partnerships, India is now Claude's #2 consumer market globally. The Anthropic-Infosys deal is the cleanest single-name lever in the operator's TRADER-65 universe.
  • Among the operator's 65-name universe, 5 names have publicly disclosed primary-engine relationships: INFOSYS (Anthropic + OpenAI), HCLTECH (multi-engine via AWS Bedrock), TCS (Google + OpenAI via Tata), PAYTM (Groq + Perplexity), BHARTIARTL (Perplexity distribution). The other 10 AI-relevant names in the universe are UNDISCLOSED.
  • Yotta Data Services is the cleanest unlisted compute beneficiary with a 12-month IPO timeline; deserves IPO-WATCH inclusion. Reliance Industries is the largest single-name AI-infra capex programme ($110B over multi-year) and is currently NOT in TRADER-65 — worth a reserve-list review.
  • MAPMYINDIA Drive GPT engine is NOT publicly disclosed, despite the brand name implying a transformer. The earlier framing in the brief that suggested an Anthropic relationship is not supported by current public evidence — the only Anthropic-MAPMYINDIA link in the source corpus is the ServiceNow (US) Anthropic-design-partner pattern in 260421_theaiportfolios_claude-portfolio-servicenow.md, NOT a MAPMYINDIA-Anthropic relationship.   • The "sovereign LLM" claim deserves nuance. Sarvam-105B (from-scratch on 12T tokens) and BharatGen Param2 17B MoE are the cleanest sovereign builds. Sarvam-M (Mistral-Small fine-tune) and Krutrim-2 (Mistral-NeMo) are derivatives — domestic labs but not sovereign architectures.
