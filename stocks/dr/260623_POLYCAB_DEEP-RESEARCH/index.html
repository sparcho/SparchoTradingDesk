Executive Summary

Polycab India is the country's largest wires & cables maker (~26-28% organised share) — the "picks-and-shovels" of T&D grid, data-center, housing, and renewables capex. FY26 was a record: consolidated revenue ₹28,884 Cr (+29%), PAT ₹2,708 Cr (+32%), EBITDA ₹4,006 Cr (+35%, ~13.9% margin) (scanx/constructionworld/investywise, FY26). W&C +33% to ₹25,179 Cr; FMEG +25% to ₹2,069 Cr; exports ₹1,570 Cr (+16.7%, 94 countries); EPC won ~₹8,000 Cr under BharatNet Phase-III. Net cash rose to ₹4,190 Cr (from ₹2,460 Cr); ₹47 dividend; market share +3-4%; described as the 4th consecutive year as the most profitable in Indian electricals.

CORE VERDICT. Polycab is the quality leader of the cables cohort — #1 share, net cash (₹4,190 Cr), ~20%+ ROCE, and exposure to multiple capex cycles (T&D + data-center + housing + renewables) at once. The "assurance from the nature of the work" is the entrenched scale + brand + distribution + balance-sheet strength. Honest caveats: (1) cable margins are structurally ~14% EBITDA (commodity-linked to copper/aluminium), so it's a volume + share + mix story; (2) the valuation is a premium (~38-42× PE historically — verify live). The load-bearing question is whether the multi-cycle capex demand sustains 20%+ growth at stable margins. Best-quality cable franchise; premium price + commodity-margin are the constraints.

3.1 Why the USP is real

3.2 Honest scepticism — five open questions
