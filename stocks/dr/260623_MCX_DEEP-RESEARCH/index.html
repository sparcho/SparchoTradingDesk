Executive Summary

MCX is India's near-monopoly commodity-derivatives exchange — ~95.9% of commodity futures, ~100% precious metals, 99.6% energy, 99.8% base metals. FY26 was a record: total income ₹2,429 Cr (+101%), EBITDA ₹1,774 Cr (73% margin, from 63%), PAT ₹1,332 Cr (+138%); Q4 PAT +291% (scanx/investywise/multibagg, FY26). The surge was bullion-volume-led — FY26 ADT ₹5.4 lakh Cr (+145%), gold/silver F&O ADT +496%, traded clients 20.9 lakh (from 13 lakh). Market cap ₹72,493 Cr (+73% in 1yr) at ~54× P/E, ~25.5× book.

CORE VERDICT. MCX is the highest-quality "assurance from the nature of the work" name in the batch — a near-pure commodity-derivatives monopoly with a 73% EBITDA margin, net cash, and a post-tech-migration cost base that delivers extreme operating leverage. The honest caveats: (1) FY26's +138% PAT was bullion-volume-led, and bullion F&O volumes are cycle/price-sensitive (a gold/silver volatility lull would slow growth); (2) ~54× PE / ~30× sales prices continued volume strength; (3) regulatory/STT and the (small) options-monopoly-scrutiny tail. The load-bearing question is the durability of bullion + broader-commodity volumes. Best franchise + margins in the batch; volume-cyclicality + premium are the constraints.

Section 3 / The Monopoly USP — Durable or Volume-Cyclical?

Variant-perception crux. Consensus: a structural commodity-derivatives monopoly compounding on financialisation + options. Variant question: how much of +138% PAT is durable vs a bullion-volatility-driven peak?

3.1 Why the USP is real

A ~96%-share, regulated, asset-light monopoly with 73% EBITDA + net cash is an exceptional, hard-to-replicate franchise; options + new products + participation growth are structural tailwinds.

3.2 Honest scepticism — five open questions

  1. Bullion-volume durability. +496% bullion F&O ADT — peak or new base?
  2. Cycle sensitivity. A gold/silver volatility lull slows volumes.
  3. Valuation. ~54× PE / ~30× sales on volume-led earnings.
  4. Regulation/STT. Commodity-derivatives policy/tax actions.
  5. New-product reliance. Growth beyond bullion (energy/metals/index).
USP VERDICT. Exceptional monopoly franchise — the question is purely the durability of the (bullion-led) volume surge and the premium multiple, not the moat.
