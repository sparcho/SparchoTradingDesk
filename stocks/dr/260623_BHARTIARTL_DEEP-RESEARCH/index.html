Executive Summary

Bharti Airtel is India's premium #2 telecom (the Jio-Airtel duopoly) + Airtel Africa + digital/business + data-centres (Nxtra). FY26: revenue ₹2,10,973 Cr (+22%), reported PAT ₹26,695 Cr (−20.45% — distorted by a FY25 one-off base); Q4 revenue ₹55,383 Cr (+15.7%), ARPU ₹257 (from ₹245), India mobile subs 37.32 cr (sahi/business-standard, FY26). EBITDA margin ~54%; ₹24 dividend. Market cap ~₹11.6 lakh cr at ~84.6× P/E; promoter 53.5%.

CORE VERDICT. Airtel is a premium-quality telecom duopoly with genuinely strong operating momentum — +22% revenue, rising ARPU (₹257, tariff-hike-led), ~54% EBITDA, a premium high-value subscriber base, plus Africa + digital + data-centre optionality. The honest caveats: (1) it trades at a premium ~84.6× PE — optically high because reported PAT is one-off-distorted (FY25 had a large exceptional gain → FY26 "−20%" is a base effect, not an operating decline); use ARPU/EBITDA/FCF as the cleaner gauges; (2) it's a leveraged business (spectrum + network debt); (3) MCap/Sales ~5.5×. The load-bearing question is continued ARPU expansion (tariff hikes) + de-leveraging. High-quality duopoly with real momentum; the premium multiple + leverage + PAT-optics noise are the constraints.
