Executive Summary

KEI Industries is India's #2 wires & cables maker (after Polycab) — power cables (incl. EHV), house wires, stainless-steel wire — riding T&D, data-center, and infra capex. FY26: revenue ₹11,748 Cr (+20.66%), PAT ₹918 Cr (+31.88%), EBITDA margin 11.81%, PAT margin 7.82% (investywise/cablecommunity, FY26); order book ~₹3,585 Cr. C&W ₹11,220 Cr; EPC ₹561 Cr; SS wire ₹216 Cr. Market cap ₹53,833 Cr at ~58.6× P/E; promoter 35% (FII 27.3%, DII 25.8%). Morgan Stanley moved to Equal-weight (target ₹5,213).

CORE VERDICT. KEI is a strong #2 cable franchise with good growth (+21% rev, +32% PAT), an EHV + retail mix-upgrade, and capacity expansion to capture T&D/DC capex — a credible "picks-and-shovels" play. But it's a notch below Polycab on the quality screen: (1) thin ~11.8% margins (commodity-linked); (2) sub-50% promoter (35%) with high institutional ownership; (3) a premium ~58.6× PE (richer than Polycab) after a QIP-funded expansion. The load-bearing question is whether capacity + EHV/retail mix sustain 20%+ growth + lift margins. Solid grower; sub-50% promoter + premium multiple are the constraints, and it's the second-best cable name vs Polycab.

Section 3 / The #2-Cable USP — Mix-Upgrade or Margin-Capped?

Variant-perception crux. Consensus: a #2 cable maker re-rating on capacity + EHV/retail mix + capex demand. Variant question: thin ~11.8% margins + sub-50% promoter at ~58.6× PE — does the mix-upgrade justify a richer multiple than Polycab?

3.1 Why the USP is real

#2 scale + EHV capability + retail brand-building + capacity expansion = a credible growth engine in a multi-cycle-capex tailwind.

3.2 Honest scepticism — five open questions

  1. Margin. ~11.8% EBITDA — does EHV/retail lift it?
  2. Promoter. 35% — institution-driven; no dominant anchor.
  3. Valuation. ~58.6× PE (richer than Polycab) — justified?

  5. Commodity cycle. Copper/aluminium swings.
USP VERDICT. Solid #2 franchise with a real mix-upgrade + capacity story — but thinner quality than Polycab (sub-50% promoter, similar margin, richer multiple). The mix-upgrade must deliver.
