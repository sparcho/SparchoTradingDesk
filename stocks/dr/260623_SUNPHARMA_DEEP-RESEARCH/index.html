Executive Summary

Sun Pharma is India's largest pharmaceutical company — global specialty (branded) + generics. FY26: net sales ~₹56,000 Cr (Q4 ₹14,612 Cr, +12.76%), Q4 PAT ₹2,714 Cr (+26.24%), ~28% EBITDA margin (sahi/marketsmojo, FY26). Specialty (Ilumya/Cequa/Odomzo/Unloxcyt) now exceeds US generics, but specialty growth decelerated to ~15% CC and Ilumya faces biosimilar competition from 2026-27. Sun agreed to acquire Organon for ~$11.75bn (cnbc). Promoter 54.5%; market cap ₹4,33,728 Cr at ~38× P/E — the highest among large-cap Indian pharma.

CORE VERDICT. Sun Pharma is the quality pharma leader — India's largest, with a high-margin global specialty franchise (innovative drugs now > US generics), ~28% EBITDA, net cash (pre-Organon), and majority promoter. The honest constraints: (1) it trades at the highest large-cap-pharma multiple (~38× PE); (2) Ilumya — a specialty anchor — faces a biosimilar cliff from 2026-27, and specialty growth has decelerated to ~15% CC (below the 20-25% embedded in bullish targets); (3) the $11.75bn Organon acquisition is a large M&A bet (integration + funding). The load-bearing question is whether new specialty launches (Unloxcyt + pipeline) + Organon offset the Ilumya cliff while justifying the premium. Quality + specialty; premium multiple + Ilumya cliff + big M&A are the constraints.
