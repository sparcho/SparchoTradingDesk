Executive Summary

HFCL is an optical-fiber-cable (OFC) + telecom-equipment company scaling exports and expanding into defence. FY26: consolidated revenue ₹4,949 Cr (+21.77%), EBITDA ₹827 Cr (~16.7% margin), PAT ₹329 Cr (+90%); Q4 revenue ₹1,824 Cr (+128% on an export surge) (whalesbook/indiaipo/investing, FY26). Order book ₹21,200 Cr (all-time high, 58% export), anchored by a USD 1.1bn 5-year global OFC contract (~50% of OFC capacity); exports jumped to 41.36% of revenue (from 12.23%), with >70% of cable production exported. A defence segment (₹2,230 Cr visibility, incl. an aerospace-platform acquisition MoU) is a new engine; a ₹580 Cr preform facility deepens backward integration. Market cap ₹31,247 Cr (+156% in 1yr) at ~95× P/E; promoter 28.3%. FY27 guide: ~20-25% revenue growth.

CORE VERDICT. HFCL has a real, improving USP — an export-led OFC franchise (the USD 1.1bn contract + ₹21,200 Cr OB is genuine visibility) + backward integration (preform) + a defence-expansion optionality. The +90% PAT + export surge are strong. Honest caveats: (1) thin ~16.7% margins (OFC is competitive/commodity-ish); (2) low promoter holding (28.3%); (3) a rich ~95× PE after a +156% one-year run; (4) the defence expansion is early (MoU stage). The load-bearing question is whether the export OFC + defence ramp sustain 20%+ growth + lift margins to justify the multiple. Improving franchise + strong OB; low promoter + thin margin + rich price are the constraints.
