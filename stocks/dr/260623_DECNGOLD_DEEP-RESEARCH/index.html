Executive Summary

Deccan Gold Mines is India's listed gold-exploration/development company. The honest headline: it is pre-production — FY26 had negligible revenue (Q4 ~₹0.59 Cr) + losses; there are no meaningful current earnings. Its value rests entirely on the option of gold production commencing: the Altyn Tor (Kyrgyzstan) project is nearing commissioning (mid-2026), and the flagship Jonnagiri (Andhra Pradesh) is "due to commence", with a stated ambition of >1 ton of gold/year. The stock is up +79.5% in a year (on the production-commencement narrative + the gold-price rally); market cap ₹4,493 Cr, P/B ~9×; promoter 20.6%.

CORE VERDICT. Deccan Gold is the most speculative name in the entire program — a pre-production gold developer with no current earnings, whose ₹4,493 Cr market cap (+79.5% in a year, P/B ~9×) is entirely an option on gold production commencing (Altyn Tor + Jonnagiri) + the gold-price rally. The screen FAILs on essentially every fundamental rule because there are no fundamentals to screen yet. This is a development-stage / junior-miner option, NOT an earnings or value story — binary-ish on project commissioning + gold prices, with low promoter holding (20.6%) and the usual mining-development execution/financing/permitting risks. The load-bearing question is whether the projects actually reach commercial production on schedule (mid-2026 Altyn Tor) and at what economics. Pure development-stage speculation — handle with that framing; sized, if at all, as a small option, not a position.
