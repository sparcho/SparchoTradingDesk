Executive Summary

Hindalco Industries is India's largest aluminium producer (integrated, first-quartile cost) + owner of Novelis (the world's largest aluminium flat-rolled/recycler). FY26: record consolidated revenue ₹2,74,944 Cr (+15%), all-time-high EBITDA ₹38,097 Cr, India-business EBITDA at a historic ₹22,671 Cr; but full-year PAT fell 16.3% to ₹13,391 Cr (from ₹16,002 Cr) on Novelis Oswego one-time exceptional costs — pre-exceptional PAT rose 10% to ₹18,733 Cr (hindalco/businessworld, FY26). Q4 PAT fell 51% (Oswego). Net debt/EBITDA rose to 1.83× (from 1.06×) on growth capex; dividend ₹5/sh. The stock is the cheapest-on-sales name in the program (~0.58× MCap/Sales; ~11-13× PE).

CORE VERDICT. Hindalco is the cheap, large-cap value metals name — India's integrated, low-cost aluminium leader + Novelis (the global FRP champion), with a record India business and the cheapest MCap/Sales in the entire program (~0.58×). The honest caveats: (1) FY26 group PAT fell 16% on the Novelis Oswego one-off (pre-exceptional +10%, so the underlying was fine) + Q4 PAT −51%; (2) net debt rose to 1.83× EBITDA on growth capex; (3) margins are structurally ~14% (aluminium + FRP-converter); (4) Novelis carries US-tariff exposure. The load-bearing question is the aluminium cycle + Novelis normalising post-Oswego while de-levering. Cheap + integrated + cash-generative India business; Novelis/Oswego + tariffs + net-debt are the constraints. A value play, not a quick-mover.
