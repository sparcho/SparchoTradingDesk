Power Grid is India's central transmission monopoly (Maharatna). CORE VERDICT. Power Grid is a cheap, high-margin, high-dividend regulated transmission monopoly — a ~88%-EBITDA annuity with a ₹1.70 lakh cr works-in-hand and a re-accelerating capex cycle (₹40,000 Cr FY26) driven by renewables-integration + HVDC + inter-regional grid demand. It is the cross-thesis "T&D backbone" (Energy/DC/Defence-Rail) at a value multiple (PE ~16×). The honest constraints: regulated ROE is capped (~15%, below 20%), and reported growth is slow (+3.7%) — though the capex surge should re-accelerate asset-base growth. The load-bearing question is whether the capex re-acceleration (post a flat patch) lifts the regulated asset base + earnings. Cheap + high-margin + high-dividend monopoly with a capex tailwind; capped ROE + slow recent growth are the trade-offs. A defensive value way to play the grid/renewables/DC capex cycle.
