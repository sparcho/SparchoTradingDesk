One-line: BSE (equity exchange) · MCX (commodity exchange) · CDSL (depository) · ANGELONE (discount broker) — four different market-infra machines, ranked on growth, margins, returns, valuation and SEBI-F&O exposure. No buy/avoid call.

Three models (read first): exchanges (BSE/MCX) = high operating leverage, revenue ≈ turnover × fee, HIGH F&O sensitivity; depository (CDSL) = ~40% annuity issuer/KYC revenue tied to the stock of accounts, LOWEST F&O sensitivity; broker (ANGELONE) = volume-sensitive, ~47% of gross income is F&O brokerage, carries MTF leverage, HIGHEST F&O sensitivity.
The dominant sector risk is SEBI's F&O crackdown (lot-size hikes, weekly-expiry rationalisation, true-to-label). Exposure gradient: ANGELONE > BSE > MCX > CDSL (MCX is commodity-insulated).

PER-COMPANY (condensed)

BSE — equity exchange; ~78% of revenue derivatives-linked. FY26 rev ₹4,834cr (+50%), OPM 64%, PAT ₹2,487cr (+88%); ROE 44.8%/ROCE 58%, net cash. An active beneficiary of the rules — the Tue/Thu expiry split handed it the Thursday Sensex franchise, lifting premium-turnover share 24.4%→26.1% (notional 38%→44%) while NSE F&O fell 18%. Catalysts: Sensex Next 30 / Focused Midcap derivatives. Risk: most equity-F&O-exposed, most SEBI-headline-reactive. (BSE Q4FY26/indmoney)

MCX — commodity-derivatives monopoly (>98% share); the fundamental standout. FY26 rev ₹2,302cr (+107%), OPM 71%, PAT ₹1,332cr (+138%); ROE 56.3%/ROCE 71.4%, net cash. Options rev ₹1,398cr (+109%); bullion ADT +496%. Margin lift from exiting legacy 63moons → TCS BaNCS. Crucially commodity-insulated from the equity-F&O crackdown — highest growth + lowest equity-reg risk. 1:5 split Dec-2025. Risk: bullion-cycle concentration, platform outages. (MCX PR, 08-May)

CDSL — depository (NSDL duopoly); the defensive annuity (~40% sticky issuer/KYC). FY26 income ₹1,239cr (+3%), PAT ₹455cr (−14%) on cost creep; ROE 24.5%, 58% payout, net cash. Demat accounts 18.01cr (first past 18cr), but incremental share softening 93%→82% as freshly-listed NSDL (IPO Aug-2025) sharpens retail focus. Lowest F&O exposure. Risk: NSDL competition, margin compression, SEBI tariff overhang. (CDSL release, 02-May)

ANGELONE — largest listed discount broker; the cyclical, leveraged, most-exposed name. FY26 income ₹5,137cr (−2%), PAT ₹915cr (−22%) — directly scarred by F&O curbs (~47% of gross income is F&O brokerage). Only one carrying real debt (₹7,951cr MTF book); cheapest (~32–35x). But visible recovery: Q1 PAT trough ₹114cr → Q4 ₹320cr (+83.5% YoY), margins 41.7% (8-qtr high) — shock absorbing. Clients ~38mn (+19.5%). Catalysts: AMC, wealth (AUM +21% QoQ), NBFC. Risk: highest F&O exposure, rising interest cost. (StockGro/Business Standard)

SEBI F&O timeline (the swing factor): Oct-2024 6-measure circular (upfront premium, ₹15L min contract, weekly-expiry→1 index/exchange, +2% ELM) phased Nov-2024→Apr-2025; true-to-label Oct-2024; Tue(NSE)/Thu(BSE) expiry Sep-2025; FutEq/delta-OI + revised MWPL Dec-2025. SYNTHESIS

Four names, three fundamentally different machines. MCX is the standout on every fundamental axis — FY26 revenue +107%, PAT +138%, 71% EBITDA margin, 56% ROE, 71% ROCE — the purest expression of exchange operating leverage, supercharged by an options-volume explosion and the one-off margin lift from finally exiting the legacy 63moons platform; crucially, as a commodity-derivatives monopoly it sits largely outside SEBI's equity-F&O crackdown, the rare combination of highest growth and relative regulatory insulation. BSE is the second-best operating story (+50% revenue, +88% PAT, 64% margin) and an active beneficiary of the rules — the Tuesday/Thursday expiry split handed it the Thursday Sensex franchise, lifting premium-turnover share from 24.4% to 26.1% — but with ~78% of revenue derivatives-linked it is the most equity-F&O-exposed and its stock is the most headline-reactive. CDSL is the defensive annuity — ~40% sticky issuer/KYC revenue, debt-free, 58% payout, structurally lowest F&O exposure — yet FY26 disappointed (PAT −14% on cost creep) and NSDL's freshly-listed competition is eroding its incremental account share (93%→82%). ANGELONE is the cyclical, leveraged, most-exposed name — the only one with negative FY26 growth, directly scarred by F&O curbs that hit ~47% of its gross income; it is also the only one carrying real debt (₹7,951cr MTF book) and the cheapest (~32–35x), with a visible Q4 recovery (PAT +83.5% YoY) suggesting the shock is absorbing. The clean read: the market pays monopoly multiples (55–65x) for the exchanges/depository and discounts the volume-sensitive broker. Regulatory-risk gradient, most→least: ANGELONE > BSE > MCX > CDSL.
