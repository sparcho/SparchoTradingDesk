Executive Summary

Astra Microwave Products is one of India's few independent RF/microwave subsystem houses — radar front-ends, missile seekers, EW and space/communication RF — a genuinely scarce domestic capability. FY26: revenue ₹1,156 Cr standalone (+10.7%) / ₹1,163 Cr consolidated (+10.6%), with a high EBITDA margin ~28% and consolidated PAT ~₹193 Cr (+25% vs ~₹153.5 Cr FY25) (investywise/marketsmojo/scanx, FY26 results; note: one source rendered PAT as "₹1,929 cr" — that is ₹192.9 cr, a lakhs/crore typo; the 16.6% PAT margin on ₹1,163 Cr corroborates ~₹193 Cr). Q4 PAT +126% (marketsmojo). Order book ₹2,141 Cr (Mar-26; 54% defence/PSU, 33% exports). The board has in-principle approved a demerger of the Space, Meteorology & Hydrology business (focus catalyst). Valuation has re-rated to ~70× P/E, market cap ₹13,453 Cr; promoter holding is only 6.54% (widely held).

CORE VERDICT. Astra owns a scarce RF/microwave defence USP with strong margins (~28% EBITDA) and a ~1.8× book-to-sales order backlog — the "assurance from the nature of the work" is real and defence-critical. But two honest caveats: (1) recent top-line growth is modest (+10.7%), so the ~70× P/E is paying for order-book conversion not delivered growth; and (2) the 6.54% promoter holding means there's no founder-anchor — the stock is institution/float-driven. The load-bearing question is whether the ₹2,141 Cr OB + demerger-driven focus accelerate revenue into the multiple. Quintessential USP, but priced ahead of the current growth rate.

Section 3 / The RF/Microwave USP — Scarce Moat or Priced-Ahead?

Variant-perception crux. Consensus: a scarce RF defence play on the indigenisation + export supercycle, hence the premium. Variant question: with +10.7% FY26 revenue, is 70× PE paying for growth that must still show up?

3.1 Why the USP is real

RF/microwave seeker/radar capability is defence-critical, export-credible, and hard to replicate domestically; 28% EBITDA margins evidence pricing power.

3.2 Honest scepticism — five open questions

  1. Conversion pace. OB is ₹2,141 Cr but FY26 revenue grew only ~11% — when does the backlog convert faster?
  2. Lumpy defence ordering. Revenue can be uneven quarter-to-quarter.
  3. Demerger value. Does the Space/Met/Hydrology demerger create or fragment value?
  4. Ownership/no-anchor. 6.54% promoter — governance/strategic-direction dependence on the board.
  5. Valuation. 70× PE on ~11% growth needs acceleration.
USP VERDICT. Genuinely scarce and high-margin — but the investment case hinges on order-book conversion accelerating revenue beyond ~11%. The moat is not the question; the growth-vs-multiple gap is.
