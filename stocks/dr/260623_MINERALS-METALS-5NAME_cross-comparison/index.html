SYNTHESIS

Two axes split the cohort: single-commodity PSU dividend/value plays (GMDC, MOIL, HINDCOPPER) vs diversified/quality compounders (HINDZINC, VEDL). HINDZINC is the standout on every quality metric — 54% EBITDA, ~70% ROCE, ~77% ROE at a record-low zinc cost while silver became roughly half of incremental profit — yet trades at only ~17x because the overhangs (parent demerger, GoI stake, HZL's own split) cloud the story rather than the fundamentals. HINDCOPPER offers the sharpest cyclical leverage (rev +49%, PAT +97% on an extraordinary copper tape) but at ~53x is the cohort's most expensive — effectively an option on flawless 4.2→12.2 MTPA execution. The PSU value bucket diverges: GMDC (net-cash + rare-earth optionality) and MOIL (debt-free, ~53% manganese share, Apr price hike) are balance-sheet fortresses, but FY26 earnings actually fell — GMDC's +45% PAT is a GST one-off, MOIL's PAT dropped 30% with no final dividend. VEDL is now a leveraged zinc/critical-minerals residual holdco whose multiples aren't yet cleanly observable. Commodity-cycle risk: all five are price-takers — copper most benefits HINDCOPPER/VEDL, zinc-silver drives HINDZINC, manganese (MOIL) and lignite (GMDC) are the softest tape. HINDZINC pairs the best balance sheet with the deepest cycle exposure; VEDL carries the cycle plus leverage.
