Executive Summary

FY26: AUM ₹3.02 lakh cr (+14.9%), Q4 PAT ₹3,021 Cr (+41%), ROA 3.63%, ROE ~19% (Q4), NIM 8.61% (stable — fattest in cohort), GNPA 4.58% (highest in cohort) (univest/icicidirect, FY26). CORE VERDICT. Shriram is the deep-value / higher-credit-risk lender of the cohort — a hard-to-replicate used-CV franchise that earns the fattest NIM (8.61%) to compensate for the highest GNPA (4.58%), at the cheapest multiple (P/B 2.23×). The honest framing: the discount is earned by the used-CV borrower base (higher structural credit risk), not a mispricing — but it's also the cohort's re-rating candidate if credit cost stays contained. The load-bearing question is whether the CV cycle behaves (GNPA contained) — a downturn spikes credit cost on this book. Cheapest + fat NIM + deep franchise; highest GNPA + CV-cycle sensitivity are the trade-offs. The value lender of the NBFC cohort.
