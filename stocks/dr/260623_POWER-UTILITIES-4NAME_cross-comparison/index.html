One-line: comparative positioning of NTPC · POWERGRID · TATAPOWER · POWERINDIA across model, growth, returns, leverage, valuation and pipeline. No buy/avoid call.

Read first — heterogeneous cohort. NTPC (regulated thermal gen) and POWERGRID (regulated transmission monopoly) are CERC cost-plus RoE PSUs — annuity profiles. TATAPOWER is an integrated private utility (gen + T&D + renewables + solar mfg + EV) — higher growth, higher leverage. POWERINDIA (Hitachi Energy India) is grid equipment (HVDC/transformers/automation) — a capital-goods/order-book business, not a utility, on a completely different multiple. PER-COMPANY (condensed)

NTPC — largest generator, CERC ~15.5% RoE on regulated equity; GoI 51.1%. FY26 rev ~flat, PAT ₹27,546 cr (+15%, one-off tax credit). >90 GW installed (crossed 90 GW 18-May; +9.6 GW FY26 record); coal PLF 72% vs RoI 63%. Pipeline: ~32 GW under construction, 149 GW by 2032 incl 60 GW RE, NGEL ramp, EDF nuclear MoU. ROE 12.1–12.4%, ROCE 8–10%. Cheapest on every multiple. Risk: CERC RoE compression, earnings-quality, leverage. (NTPC PR/SolarQuarter/Kotak, May 2026)

POWERGRID — inter-state transmission monopoly; GoI 51.3%. FY26 total income ₹47,684 cr (+0.5%), EBITDA margin ~83%, PAT ₹15,928 cr (+3%). Network 1,84,960 ckm, availability 99.84%. Works-in-hand ~₹1.70 lakh cr; FY26 capex ₹39,967 cr (beat); LT opportunity ₹6.6 lakh cr to FY32 on RE-evacuation. ROE 15.85%. Highest yield + margin. Risk: flat core revenue, TBCB competition (won 9/28 FY26), RoE revision. (PSU Watch, 15-May)

TATAPOWER — largest integrated private utility; gen+T&D+RE+solar mfg+EV. FY26 rev ₹62,429 cr (flat to −5%), PAT ₹5,118 cr (+7%); RE PAT +59%, solar-mfg PAT +103%. 16.7 GW installed (~47% clean); 4.3 GW cell + 4.3 GW module mfg (Tirunelveli). ~₹1.46 lakh cr capex FY25–30 (~60% RE), 23 GW RE by FY30. Heaviest leverage (D/E ~1.6x, Debt/EBITDA ~6x, FCF −₹7,677 cr). ~34x P/E. Risk: leverage, Mundra under-recovery, execution. (Tata Power Q4/pv-magazine)

POWERINDIA (Hitachi Energy India) — grid equipment (HVDC/transformers/automation/FACTS); Hitachi 71.3%; not a utility. FY26 rev ₹8,148 cr (+27.6%), PAT ₹988 cr (+157%); Q4 EBITDA 16.4% (structural expansion from ~4–7% FY23). Record backlog ₹29,555 cr (+53.5%), book-to-bill ~2.27x; Q4 orders led by data centres + rail/metro; exports 36.8%. Net cash post-QIP. ROCE 19.4% (29% TTM). Catalysts: grid/HVDC supercycle, data-centre demand. But 166–180x P/E prices in years of flawless delivery against lumpy orders. (Hitachi Energy FY26, May 2026)

SYNTHESIS

Three archetypes, and the numbers reflect them. NTPC and POWERGRID are regulated-return PSUs whose earnings track a CERC-set RoE on a growing equity base, not market volumes — hence near-flat FY26 revenue yet steady PAT. They are the value/yield anchors: NTPC is the cheapest name on every multiple (~12–13x P/E, ~1.8x P/B, 10.9x EV/EBITDA); POWERGRID offers the highest yield (~3.1–3.2%), the fattest margin (~83% — a transmission-annuity signature) and the best regulated ROE (15.85%). Both carry moderate PSU leverage backstopped by annuity cash flows; their shared risk is regulatory (any CERC RoE revision compresses the model). TATAPOWER is the integrated-private growth story — a fast-scaling 11.6 GW renewables book and a high-margin solar-manufacturing line — but that optionality costs ~34x and comes with the cohort's heaviest leverage (D/E ~1.6x, Debt/EBITDA ~6x, negative FCF), a different risk axis from the PSUs'. POWERINDIA is not a utility at all — a grid-equipment maker riding India's transmission/HVDC/data-centre capex supercycle, with the cohort's only real top-line growth (+28%), PAT +157%, highest ROCE, a record ₹29,555 cr backlog and a net-cash balance sheet — but at 166–180x P/E pricing years of flawless delivery against lumpy orders. Bottom line: regulated stability (NTPC/POWERGRID) vs leveraged integrated growth (TATAPOWER) vs an equipment-cycle compounder (POWERINDIA) — same theme, three different risk-return engines.
