Executive Summary

Garden Reach Shipbuilders & Engineers is a Navratna defence shipbuilder (warships, corvettes, frigates) executing a strong delivery ramp — 8 warships delivered in FY26. FY26: revenue ₹7,002 Cr, PAT ₹748 Cr (univest/trendlyne, FY26 results); Q4 PAT ₹303.19 Cr (+24.1% from ₹244.25 Cr). (Note: one web read claimed FY26 revenue/PAT "+300%/+294%" — that is implausible versus GRSE's known ~₹5,000 Cr FY25 revenue base; the realistic FY26 growth is roughly +35–40% revenue / +40% PAT off that base — the "+300%" is a data error, flagged; the absolute figures ₹7,002 Cr / ₹748 Cr are used.) Order book ₹18,482 Cr (31-Dec-25; first dip below ₹20k on high execution), with a ₹33,000 Cr Next-Gen-Corvette (NGC) high-probability opportunity and ~₹1.52 lakh Cr pipeline — management frames FY27 as a peak-revenue year. Government holds 74.5% (zero pledge); market cap ₹32,602 Cr at ~52× P/E.

CORE VERDICT. GRSE is a clean, fast-ramping Navratna shipbuilder — net cash, zero pledge, 8 deliveries in FY26, a ~₹33,000 Cr NGC catalyst, and FY27 framed as a peak year. The "assurance from the work" is real (sovereign naval demand + execution proof). Honest caveats: (1) shipbuilding margins are structurally thin (~10.7% PAT margin) so the model is volume/execution-led, not margin-led; (2) ~52× PE / ~4.7× sales is rich, and the OB dipped (fast execution must be replaced by new awards — NGC is the swing). The load-bearing question is whether the NGC + pipeline convert to keep OB and growth compounding past the FY27 peak. Clean execution story; valuation + order-replacement are the watch-items.

Section 3 / The Navratna Shipbuilder USP — Execution Ramp or Peak?

Variant-perception crux. Consensus: a Navratna shipbuilder in a naval-modernisation supercycle with strong execution + NGC optionality. Variant question: with FY27 framed as the peak and OB dipping, is growth near a plateau pending new awards?

3.1 Why the USP is real

Defence shipbuilding is a sovereign-critical, high-barrier capability; GRSE's delivery ramp (8 ships FY26) + Navratna status + export ambitions evidence execution. Naval demand is structural.

3.2 Honest scepticism — five open questions

  1. Order replacement. OB dipped <₹20k on fast execution — NGC + new awards must refill it.
  2. FY27 "peak". If FY27 is the peak, what's the FY28+ trajectory without big new orders?
  3. Thin margins. ~10.7% PAT margin — volume/execution-dependent.
  4. NGC timing/win. ₹33,000 Cr is "high-probability," not awarded — execution risk.
  5. Valuation. ~52× PE on a thin-margin, award-dependent model.
USP VERDICT. Real, clean execution franchise — but growth durability hinges on order replacement (NGC), and the model is volume-led with thin margins. The moat is sovereign; the trajectory is award-dependent.
