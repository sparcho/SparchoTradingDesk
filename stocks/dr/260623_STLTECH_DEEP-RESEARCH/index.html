Executive Summary

Sterlite Technologies (STL) is an optical-fibre-cable maker (8% global share ex-China) pivoting toward AI-data-centre infrastructure. FY26: revenue ₹4,745 Cr (+18.8%), PAT just ₹56 Cr (a turnaround from prior losses), EBITDA margin 13.2% (investywise/scanx, FY26); Q4 revenue ₹1,441 Cr (+37%), net profit ₹59 Cr. Order book ₹7,309 Cr (+67%). Net debt rose to ~₹1,504 Cr. The honest headline: the stock is up ~489% in a year on the AI-data-centre/OFC narrative + the turnaround — so the PE (~15,586×) is meaningless (near-zero earnings). Vedanta-group promoter 44.4%.

CORE VERDICT. STL is the most speculative / narrative-driven name in Batches 8-9 — a barely-profitable (₹56 Cr PAT), low-margin (13.2% EBITDA) OFC business that has re-rated +489% in a year on the AI-data-centre + OFC-recovery story. The genuine positives: revenue +18.8%, a strong order book (₹7,309 Cr, +67%), and a real (if fragile) turnaround to profit after loss-making years. The honest reality: the valuation is completely disconnected from current earnings (PE ~15,586×; MCap/Sales 6.4× on a 13.2%-margin commodity-ish business), net debt is rising (₹1,504 Cr), and it's a Vedanta-group company (44.4%). The load-bearing question is whether the AI-DC pivot + OFC recovery deliver a large earnings ramp to justify the ₹30,509 Cr cap. A turnaround + narrative bet, NOT a fundamentals-supported holding — handle with that framing. Direct comp to HFCL (which is at least solidly profitable).
