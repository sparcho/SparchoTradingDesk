Executive Summary

Graphite India is a leading domestic graphite-electrode maker (for EAF steel) — and FY26 was a deep earnings downcycle: standalone PAT ₹264 Cr (−41.6% from ₹452 Cr); consolidated PAT ₹171 Cr (−62.7% from ₹458 Cr) on roughly flat revenue (cons income ₹3,026 Cr, +0.93%), with a sharp Q4 loss (marketsmojo/scanx, FY26). The graphite + carbon segment did ₹2,594 Cr. The forward optionality: a ₹4,330 Cr board-approved investment (Jan-2026) into synthetic-graphite anode materials for Li-ion batteries. Promoter 65.34% (cash-rich balance sheet); market cap ₹14,218 Cr at ~81.7× P/E — but on collapsed earnings (not a normal multiple).

CORE VERDICT. Graphite India is a cyclical-commodity name in a deep trough with a battery-anode optionality — much like PCBL (Batch 2). The honest reality: FY26 earnings collapsed (cons PAT −62.7%, Q4 loss) as graphite-electrode prices/margins crashed on global EAF-steel + electrode-cycle weakness; the ~81.7× PE is a function of depressed earnings, not a quality premium. The genuine positives: a cash-rich balance sheet (cushions the downcycle), a 65% promoter, and a real ₹4,330 Cr Li-ion-anode pivot. The load-bearing question is whether the electrode cycle bottoms + the anode bet de-risks. This is a cyclical-recovery + optionality bet, NOT a quick-mover-with-assurance — the cushion is the cash, not the current earnings.
