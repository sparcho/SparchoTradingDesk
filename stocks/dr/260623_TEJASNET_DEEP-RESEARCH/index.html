Executive Summary

Tejas Networks is a Tata-Group telecom-equipment company with indigenously-designed 4G/5G RAN + optical/broadband products — strategically important (it powers BSNL's pan-India 4G). The honest headline: FY26 was a loss year — revenue ₹1,103 Cr with a consolidated PAT loss of ₹909 Cr (Q4 revenue ₹333 Cr, loss ₹211 Cr) (scanx/multibagg/tejasnetworks, FY26) — as the large BSNL deployment cycle rolled off and provisions hit. The order book is ₹1,514 Cr (+49% YoY) but small vs the prior BSNL scale. Strategic positives: BSNL 4G live, a NEC 5G Massive-MIMO supply contract, Americas 5G trials, and 676 cumulative patents. Tata (Panatone/Tata Sons) is the majority shareholder.

CORE VERDICT. Tejas is the clearest turnaround/story-stage name across all five batches — a strategically vital, Tata-backed indigenous 4G/5G RAN franchise (a genuine national-security/Atmanirbhar asset), but it is currently loss-making (FY26 −₹909 Cr) with revenue down post-BSNL-deployment and a small ₹1,514 Cr order book. On the fundamental screen it fails on every profitability/return rule — only Tata promoter backing passes. The bull thesis is entirely forward: new domestic + international 5G orders + the next BSNL/private-telco/export cycle re-igniting growth. The load-bearing question is order-inflow + a return to profitability. High strategic value, high risk — a recovery bet, not a quality holding today. Honest framing matters: do not read the strategic USP as current earnings quality — there are none right now.
