Executive Summary

Mahanagar Gas is Mumbai's city-gas-distribution (CGD) monopoly (CNG + PNG). FY26: consolidated revenue ₹9,185 Cr (+12.76%), PAT ₹840.55 Cr (standalone ₹846.82 Cr, −18.67%) — a decline on LNG-supply disruptions + margin pressure (higher gas costs / APM-allocation cuts) (whalesbook/psuconnect, FY26); volume 4.585 MMSCMD; 518 CNG stations. Market cap ₹11,942 Cr (−13.3% 1yr) at ~14× P/E, ~1.3× sales; promoter 32.5%; dividend ₹30/sh (high yield).

CORE VERDICT. MGL is a cheap, cash-generative CGD monopoly (Mumbai CNG/PNG) with net cash + a high dividend (₹30/sh) at ~14× PE / ~1.3× sales. The honest constraints: (1) FY26 PAT fell ~19% on gas-cost inflation + APM-allocation cuts (forcing costlier LNG) — a margin squeeze; (2) sub-50% promoter (32.5%); (3) the long-term EV threat to CNG-vehicle demand is the structural overhang. The load-bearing question is whether gas-cost/allocation normalises (margin recovery) + PNG-domestic/industrial volume offsets CNG's EV risk. Cheap + monopoly + yield; margin pressure + EV-overhang are the trade-offs. A value/yield CGD play with a margin-recovery angle + a structural CNG-vs-EV question.
