Executive Summary

Bajaj Finance is India's bellwether diversified consumer NBFC. FY26: AUM ₹5.10 lakh cr (+22%), PAT ₹19,332 Cr (+15%), GNPA 1.01% / NNPA 0.41%, ROA 4.4-4.6%, ROE 19-20%, CRAR 21.55% (Bajaj Q4/FY26 investor presentation; cross-checked scanx). 52.5M loans booked, 119M+ customer franchise. Market cap ₹5,96,695 Cr at P/B 5.24× / P/E 30.9× (19-Jun-26) — a deep premium.

CORE VERDICT. Bajaj Finance is the best-in-class quality compounder in the NBFC cohort — cleanest asset quality (GNPA 1.0%), best ROA (~4.5%), scale + tech + deposit franchise + cross-sell, growing AUM +22%. The 5.24× P/B is earned by that quality + ~19-20% ROE. The honest constraints: (1) the premium P/B (5.2×) leaves limited margin for error — any growth/quality wobble de-rates it; (2) ROE sits ~19-20% (just at the bar); (3) law-of-large-numbers deceleration on a ₹5-lakh-cr book. The load-bearing question is whether AUM compounds 20%+ at 4.5% ROA to sustain the premium. Best quality, richest price; see the NBFC comparison for the archetype contrast. The quality-compounder NBFC, priced for it.
