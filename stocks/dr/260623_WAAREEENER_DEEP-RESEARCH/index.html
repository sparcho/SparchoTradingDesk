Plain-English Glossary

Solar-manufacturing domain

TermMeaning
PV cell vs moduleA cell is the silicon wafer that converts light to electricity; a module (panel) is many cells laminated together. Module-making is lower-margin assembly; cell-making is higher-value and more technical. Waaree is integrating backwards from modules into cells (and eventually wafers/ingots).
TOPCon / mono-PERCCell technologies. Mono-PERC is the prior generation; TOPCon is the current higher-efficiency standard most new Indian capacity is building.
GW (gigawatt)Capacity unit. Waaree FY26 module capacity ~12.6 GW, cell capacity ~2.3 GW (source below).
ALMMApproved List of Models & Manufacturers — an Indian govt list; projects with govt linkage must use ALMM-listed modules (and, increasingly, ALMM-listed cells), protecting domestic makers from imports.
DCRDomestic Content Requirement — govt-scheme mandate to use Indian-made cells+modules; a demand moat for integrated domestic players.
BCDBasic Customs Duty — import duty on cells/modules (40% modules / 25% cells historically) that shields domestic manufacturing.
PLIProduction-Linked Incentive — govt subsidy for integrated giga-scale manufacturing.
Backward integrationOwning more of the chain (module → cell → wafer → ingot → polysilicon). Reduces dependence on (mostly Chinese) cell imports and lifts margin/defensibility.
Order book (OB)Contracted but not-yet-delivered revenue. Waaree's is large and export-heavy.

Executive Summary

Waaree Energies is India's largest pure-play solar-PV manufacturer and, post the FY26 print, a genuine scale leader: FY26 revenue ₹26,537 Cr (+84% YoY), PAT ₹3,884 Cr (≈2× vs ₹1,928 Cr FY25), EBITDA ₹5,909 Cr (+117%) (saurenergy/scanx, FY26 results 29-Apr-2026; cross-checked Business-Standard/Univest). CORE VERDICT. Waaree is a real, scaled, profitable leader executing the integration thesis (cells 0.1→2.3 GW in one year is the proof-point), with rare multi-year export-OB visibility. It clears the quality bar on the discipline screen (promoter skin-in, 22% margins, ~50% sales CAGR) but fails on valuation (PE ~24×, MCap/Sales ~3.4×) and leaves ROCE/ROE + exact leverage PENDING. Section 1 / Company Overview

Waaree Energies Ltd is India's largest solar-PV module manufacturer by capacity, headquartered in Mumbai, part of the Waaree Group. IPO'd October 2024 (one of the most-subscribed Indian IPOs of that cycle). The business spans module manufacturing, an expanding in-house cell line, EPC/project work, and a growing international (notably US) footprint. FY26 was a step-change year on every line.

Section 3 / Cell-Line Backward-Integration + Export Order Book — Durable Moat or Cyclical Peak?

This is the load-bearing forward question and the variant-perception vs consensus crux. Consensus: Waaree is a high-quality scale leader riding India's manufacturing + US-export tailwind — hence the premium multiple. The variant question: how much of the FY26 step-change is durable integration economics vs a cyclical peak in module ASPs and a one-time export surge.

3.1 The integration roadmap (wafer → cell → module)

Waaree is moving up the value chain from module assembly into cells (2.3 GW live) and, per capex commentary, toward wafer/ingot. Why it matters: an integrated maker (a) captures cell margin instead of importing cells (mostly from China), (b) qualifies for ALMM-cell / DCR demand that pure module-assemblers will lose as the cell-ALMM list activates, and (c) earns BCD protection on both layers. This is the structural defensibility argument.

3.3 Honest scepticism — five open questions

  1. Chinese oversupply / module ASP deflation. Global module prices have been in structural decline on Chinese overcapacity. If ASPs fall faster than volume + mix lift, FY26's 22% EBITDA margin compresses. This is the #1 exogenous risk.
  2. US policy / tariff risk. Two-thirds of the OB is export-weighted, much to the US. Any AD/CVD action, IRA rollback, or tariff change repricing US demand hits the highest-margin slice. Quantified US-revenue split PENDING.
  3. ASP / margin trajectory. FY27 EBITDA guide ₹7,000–7,700 Cr is strong, but realized module ASP per watt is the swing variable — PENDING disclosure.
  4. OB conversion + working capital. The OCF-weakening flag (saurenergy) suggests scaling is working-capital hungry. Conversion timing and receivables quality matter — PENDING balance-sheet detail.
  5. Capex funding + dilution. ₹30,000 Cr capex with ~₹10,000 Cr external funding implies possible equity issuance — a dilution watch.
MOAT VERDICT. Real and deepening — the integration is executing (cells 0.1→2.3 GW), and ALMM-cell + DCR + BCD give a genuine domestic-demand moat a pure assembler lacks. But the moat is partly cyclical: it is strongest when global ASPs are firm and US policy is friendly, and both are exogenous and mean-reverting. Treat the integration as durable structural value and the FY26 margin level as cycle-sensitive.

5.1 What current valuation implies

At ~24× trailing / ~3.4× MCap-to-Sales, the market is already pricing continued high-growth + sustained 20%+ margins. The reverse-DCF read: to justify ~₹89,900 Cr you need the FY27 EBITDA guide (₹7–7.7k Cr) to land and margins to hold ~20%+ through the next 2–3 years of OB conversion — i.e. the cyclical-peak risk does NOT materialise. The forward multiple (17× FY27E P/E, ~11× EV/EBITDA) is far more reasonable if the guidance is met.

5.2 Bull-case fair value

OB converts on schedule, US demand holds, cells scale to >5 GW, margins hold ~22% → FY27 EBITDA at/above the top of guidance (₹7,700 Cr+) and FY28 growth continues. Re-rating sustained at ~13–14× EV/EBITDA on rising EBITDA implies meaningful upside from ~₹3,125 (magnitude PENDING a full model; directionally +25–50%).

5.3 Base case

Guidance roughly met, some ASP compression offset by mix/volume; the stock compounds with earnings rather than re-rating — fair value broadly around current to modestly higher.

5.4 Bear-case fair value

Module ASP deflation accelerates + a US tariff/IRA shock cuts the export book → FY27 EBITDA below guidance, margin toward mid-teens. De-rating to ~8–9× EV/EBITDA implies material downside (magnitude PENDING; directionally −25–40%).

VALUATION VERDICT. Premium but internally consistent — Waaree is cheaper than Premier on P/E (24× vs 33×) despite ~3.4× the revenue and ~2.6× the PAT, so the premium is earned relative to the peer. The asymmetry is real but two-sided: the upside needs the cycle to behave; the downside is an exogenous ASP/policy shock. Anchor timing on FY27 margin delivery, not headline OB.
