Executive Summary

Voltas is India's #1 room-AC brand (Tata group, ~18-19% share). The honest headline: FY26 was a bad year — revenue fell ~7.6%; PAT collapsed ~55.6% to ₹370 Cr (from ₹834 Cr), with Q4 PAT down ~52% to ₹113 Cr (sahi/scanx, FY26) — on AC competitive-pricing margin pressure + EMP (projects) drag, even though AC volumes were strong (~2.25M units FY26; 1M units in Q1 FY27 alone). Market cap ₹44,802 Cr at ~114-131× P/E — extreme, because earnings are depressed. Tata group 30.30% (no pledge); dividend ₹4.

CORE VERDICT. Voltas has a genuine USP — India's #1 AC brand (~18-19% share) + strong distribution + a structural AC-penetration runway + Tata backing. But it is the weakest current-fundamentals name in the batch: FY26 revenue fell ~7.6% and PAT collapsed ~56% on AC margin pressure (intense competition) + EMP project losses, and the PE (~114-131×) is meaningless-high on depressed earnings. Volumes are strong (the brand still sells), but pricing/margins were crushed. The load-bearing question is whether AC margins normalise + EMP stops dragging — a margin-recovery bet, not a quick-mover. Strong brand + volume + structural runway; collapsed margins + extreme PE + competitive pricing are the constraints. A margin-recovery/turnaround case, with the AC competitive intensity as the structural risk.
