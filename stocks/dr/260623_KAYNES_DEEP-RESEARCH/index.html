Executive Summary

Kaynes Technology is a premium EMS company moving up the value chain into semiconductors (OSAT/ATMP) and HDI PCB. FY26: revenue ₹3,626 Cr (+33%), PAT ₹363.9 Cr (+24%), EBITDA margin 15.8% (constructionworld/tradingview/investywise, FY26); order book ₹8,366 Cr (~1.5 years). Strategically, its Sanand OSAT facility delivered India's first commercially manufactured multichip module — a genuine national-first; the HDI PCB unit is nearing readiness. The honest headline: the stock is down ~44.5% over the past year even though revenue/PAT grew — a valuation + execution-credibility reset (the name had run to extreme multiples and faced working-capital/credibility questions). Promoter 53.5%; market cap ₹21,807 Cr at ~60× P/E, ~4.6× book.

CORE VERDICT. Kaynes has the most differentiated USP in the EMS batch — premium EMS (15.8% EBITDA, double the commodity EMS names) plus a real semiconductor back-end (OSAT) and PCB integration, with ~1.5 years of order visibility. But it's the batch's clearest case of "great story, painful price action": −44.5% in a year as the market reset an over-extended multiple and questioned execution/working-capital. The load-bearing question is whether OSAT/PCB scale profitably (capital-heavy, execution-intensive) and whether ~60× PE is now a fair entry after the de-rate. High-USP, high-capex, high-multiple — the de-rate has improved (not fixed) the risk/reward.

3.2 Honest scepticism — five open questions
