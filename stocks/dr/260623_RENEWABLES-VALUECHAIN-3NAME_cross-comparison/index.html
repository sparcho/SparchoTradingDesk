PER-COMPANY (condensed)

WAAREE — the manufacturer. India's largest module maker, largest non-Chinese globally. FY26 rev ₹26,537 cr (+84%), EBITDA ₹5,909 cr (OPM ~22%), PAT ₹3,884 cr (+100%) — fully cross-verified. ROE 32.8%, ROCE 38.8% — cleanest fundamentals of the trio, at ~23–24x. Module cap ~15 GW (sources dispute 12/15/20.4/22.3 GW — PENDING), cell production leapt 0.1→2.3 GW; order book ~₹53,000 cr; ~33% overseas. Broke ground Mar-2026 on a 10 GW ingot+wafer complex (~₹6,200 cr). Catalyst: ALMM List-II (cells) from 1-Jun-2026 = domestic-content tailwind. Risks: US AD/CVD (prelim 126%; mgmt says not applicable as it uses non-Chinese cells for US, but ~33% overseas is the exposure); negative FCF −₹3,209 cr; valuation. (Saur Energy/pv-magazine)

ADANIGREEN — the operator/IPP. Pure-play renewable IPP; ~91% EBITDA margin on 19.3 GW operational (+35%), Khavda mega-park 9.4 GW (→30 GW by 2029), target 50 GW by 2030; FY26 generation = 106% of contracted. FY26 power-supply rev ₹11,602 cr (+22%); reported PAT ~₹1,650 cr (derived; Q3 was a −₹41 cr loss) — AGEL headlines "cash profit" ₹5,399 cr. The balance-sheet-risk name: net debt ₹91,252 cr, ~5x net-debt/EBITDA; P/E ~150x only makes sense on EV/EBITDA (~27–31x) + growth. Governance overhang substantially resolved May-2026 (US DOJ moved to dismiss with prejudice 18-May; SEC civil settled $18M, no admission). Risk: leverage/financing, grid curtailment (lost est. ₹1,200–1,500 cr EBITDA FY26), valuation. (AGEL Q4/Whalesbook/Business Standard)

IREDA — the lender. Largest pure-play green-financing NBFC; GoI 71.76%; Navratna; RBI IFC status. FY26 income ₹8,309 cr (+23%), loan book ₹92,980 cr (+22%), PAT ₹1,873 cr (+10%), NIM 3.65% (single-source, flagged). Asset quality: GNPA 3.49% (rose to 4.13% mid-FY26 on wind stress, recovered), NNPA 1.29%, CRAR 20.59%. ROE ~15.6–16%, D/E ~5.5x. Trades ~2.5x P/B / 18.5x P/E vs PFC's ~1.1x / ~5.7x — premium rests on growth, not asset quality (PFC GNPA ~1.3%, ROE 21%) — and has already de-rated ~29% over 1 year. Risk: asset quality/concentration, equity-dilution (QIPs), cost of funds. (SolarQuarter/Screener/ICICIdirect)

SYNTHESIS

These three let an investor touch the same 500-GW-by-2030 build-out through three structurally different P&Ls — complements, not substitutes. Waaree, the manufacturer, is levered to module price × volume and to trade policy: cleanest fundamentals of the trio (rev +84%, PAT +100%, ROE 32.8%, ROCE 38.8%) at a comparatively modest ~23–24x. Its tailwind (domestic ALMM List-II from 1-Jun) and its threat (US AD/CVD on ~33% overseas) are two sides of the same policy coin; watch the negative FCF as capex scales. Adani Green, the operator, monetises tariffs and cost of capital, earning ~91% EBITDA on 19.3 GW — but it is the balance-sheet-risk name (~₹91,252 cr net debt, ~5x net-debt/EBITDA, P/E ~150x that only computes on EV/EBITDA + growth). Crucially it benefits from cheap modules, so its interest partly opposes the manufacturer's protectionist tailwind; the May-2026 DOJ/SEC resolution removed a major overhang. IREDA, the lender, monetises loan-book growth and asset quality — the most diversified, technology-agnostic exposure — but trades at ~2.5x P/B / 18.5x vs PFC's ~1.1x / ~5.7x, a premium resting on growth rather than returns or asset quality (GNPA 3.49% vs PFC ~1.3%). Net: highest quality/growth combo = Waaree; highest operating scale + leverage = Adani Green; broadest theme proxy with a valuation/credit watch = IREDA.
