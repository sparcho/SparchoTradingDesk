Executive Summary

MOIL Limited is India's largest manganese-ore producer — a Miniratna PSU with ~53% domestic share (a near-monopoly), net cash. FY26: revenue ₹1,472.84 Cr, PBT ₹337.84 Cr, PAT ₹267.48 Cr — a soft year as ore realisations weakened (Q1 PAT −66%, Q3 −30%), and the Board declared no final dividend (indianpsu/screener, FY26). Production grew to 19.07 lakh MT (from 18.03) but sales were flat (15.89 lakh MT) — volume up, price down. GoI 64.68%; market cap ₹6,029 Cr at ~22.5× P/E. Capacity target: 3.5 million tonnes by 2030.

CORE VERDICT. MOIL is a net-cash manganese-ore monopoly (~53% share) — a structurally strong franchise (low debt, dominant position, steel-input demand). But FY26 was a soft year: weak ore realisations dragged profit (Q1 −66%, Q3 −30%), and the Board skipped the final dividend — i.e. the current momentum is poor even as production grew. At ~22.5× PE on depressed earnings, it's not cheap on this year's numbers. The load-bearing question is the manganese-price / steel-cycle recovery + the volume-growth path to 3.5 MT by 2030. Monopoly + net cash; weak realisations + steel-cycle dependence are the constraints. A cyclical-recovery name, not a quick-mover today.
