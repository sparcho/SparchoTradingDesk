SYNTHESIS — where each stands

The cohort splits into large-cap quality compounders (HAL, BEL, MAZDOCK, GRSE) and richly-valued small-caps (ASTRAMICRO, PARAS).

HAL is the cheapest name on the deepest visibility — a 7.7x book-to-bill (₹2.54 lakh cr backlog) at just 31x earnings — but sub-10% growth and GE-engine dependence explain the discount and the ~15% one-year de-rating. GRSE is the growth-and-returns leader: +38% revenue, +42% PAT and cohort-best 43% ROCE, with the ~₹33,000 cr Next-Gen Corvette award as the near-term backlog catalyst — though its ~11% margin is structurally thinnest and earnings are Navy-concentrated. BEL is the most balanced — 16% growth, ~29% margins, 36.5% ROCE, 2.7x book-to-bill, explicit >₹55,000 cr FY27 inflow guidance — but at 49x P/E and 12.5x book it is no longer cheap. MAZDOCK pairs strong returns (29% ROE) with the thinnest current book-to-bill (1.6x); it is effectively a binary call on the P-75I submarine award, which would transform its backlog.

The small-caps look expensive relative to growth and returns: PARAS (~129x P/E, 17% ROCE, undisclosed order book) and ASTRAMICRO (~73–82x P/E, 20% ROCE) are the valuation outliers with high working-capital intensity. ASTRAMICRO at least delivers cohort-top margins (~29%) and a demerger catalyst; PARAS trades on optics-monopoly scarcity rather than current returns.

One-liner per name: HAL = cheapest + deepest backlog, growth-light · BEL = balanced compounder, fully valued · GRSE = growth/ROCE leader, NGC catalyst, thin margin · MAZDOCK = P-75I binary, thin current book · ASTRAMICRO = high margin + demerger, pricey vs ROCE · PARAS = niche monopoly, dearest, opaque book.
