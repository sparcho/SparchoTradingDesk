Executive Summary

Paytm (One97 Communications) is a payments + financial-services-distribution platform. FY26 was its first full profitable year: operating revenue ₹8,437 cr (+22%), PAT ₹552 cr (from a −₹663 cr loss), EBITDA +₹502 cr (from −₹1,506 cr) (asianetnews/business-standard, FY26). Merchant GMV ₹6.5 lakh cr (+27%); FS-distribution ₹2,593 cr (+52%, repeat borrowers >50% of merchant loans); merchant subscriptions 1.51 cr. Market cap ₹69,730 cr; net cash ₹13,315 cr (highest ever).

CORE VERDICT. Paytm is a freshly-profitable payments platform turnaround — FY26 was its first profitable year (PAT ₹552 cr, EBITDA flipped positive), driven by AI-led cost efficiency + GMV growth (+27%) + FS-distribution scaling (+52%), all on a large net-cash cushion (₹13,315 cr). The honest framing: it is priced on platform optionality (payments scale + lending-distribution + super-app), NOT on a lending book or current returns — the multiple reflects the future, not the ₹552 cr PAT. Caveats: (1) profitability is partly cost-cut-led — revenue durability + take-rate are the test; (2) regulatory overhang (UPI economics, DLG/lending-distribution rules, prior PPBL episode); (3) competition (PhonePe/GPay). The load-bearing question is durable, growing profitability + FS-distribution scaling against regulation. First-profit + net cash + optionality; regulatory + profitability-durability are the constraints.
