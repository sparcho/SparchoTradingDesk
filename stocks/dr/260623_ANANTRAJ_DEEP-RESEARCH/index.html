Executive Summary

Anant Raj Limited is a Gurugram-focused real-estate developer pivoting into data centers. FY26: revenue ₹2,511.6 Cr (+21.92%), EBITDA ₹723.15 Cr (+35.94%, ~28.8% margin), PAT ₹557.02 Cr (+30.81%) (investywise/multibagg, FY26); cash rose to ₹899 Cr (from ₹330 Cr), total assets ₹6,868 Cr. The DC pivot: data-center/allied revenue ₹176.49 Cr FY26; 28 MW operational IT load (Manesar 21 + Panchkula 7), with a target of 357 MW by 2032 backed by a ₹20,000 Cr planned investment. Real estate (Sector 63A luxury) remains the cash engine. Promoter (Sarin family) ~60%; valuation premium (PE ~40-45× historically — verify live).

CORE VERDICT. Anant Raj is the "real-asset / data-center" leg of this batch — a profitable, low-debt RE developer (28.8% EBITDA, +31% PAT) with a high-growth data-center optionality (28 MW → 357 MW by 2032). Unlike the cyclical commodity names in the batch (Graphite, AREM), it has genuine current earnings + a clean balance sheet (₹899 Cr cash). The honest caveats: (1) the DC business is small today (₹176 Cr of ₹2,512 Cr) — the 357 MW / ₹20,000 Cr ambition is large vs the current scale, so funding + execution are the swing; (2) RE is cyclical + Gurugram-micro-market-concentrated; (3) the valuation is a premium. The load-bearing question is whether the DC ramp converts the optionality into earnings while the RE base funds it. Profitable base + real DC optionality; DC-capex funding/execution + premium price are the constraints.

Section 3 / The RE-Base + DC-Optionality USP — Growth Pivot or Over-Ambition?

Variant-perception crux. Consensus: a profitable RE developer re-rating on a data-center growth pivot. Variant question: DC is small today (₹176 Cr) — is 357 MW / ₹20,000 Cr realistic vs the current scale?

3.1 Why the USP is real

A profitable RE base (~29% EBITDA, land bank) + ₹899 Cr cash funds the DC build; Indian DC demand (AI/cloud/data-localisation) is a structural tailwind; owned land lowers DC land cost.

3.2 Honest scepticism — five open questions

  1. DC scale vs ambition. ₹176 Cr today vs 357 MW / ₹20,000 Cr by 2032 — funding + execution?
  2. Funding/dilution. ₹20,000 Cr is large vs current assets (₹6,868 Cr).
  3. RE cyclicality. Gurugram-micro-market concentration.
  4. DC competition. Hyperscalers + established DC operators (Nxtra/CtrlS/STT).
  5. Valuation. Premium multiple on early-stage DC optionality.
USP VERDICT. A profitable RE base with a genuine, well-located DC optionality — but the 357 MW ambition is large vs today's scale; funding/execution is the real question, not the concept.
