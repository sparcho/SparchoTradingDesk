Executive Summary

Angel One is India's largest retail discount broker by client base — 37.4 million clients, ~16.7% demat market share — diversifying into wealth, AMC, and distribution (a fintech super-app). FY26: consolidated total income ₹5,152 Cr, PAT ₹915 Cr (−21.9% YoY) — a down year driven by SEBI's F&O curbs that compressed retail derivatives volumes; but Q4 recovered strongly — PAT ₹320 Cr (+83.5%) on revenue ₹1,459 Cr (+38.2%) (business-standard/stockgro, FY26). Market cap ₹32,305 Cr at ~35.3× P/E (cheapest in Batch 4); promoter 28.8%.

CORE VERDICT. Angel One is a genuine scale franchise (37.4M clients, 16.7% demat share) with a credible diversification beyond pure broking (wealth + AMC + distribution) — and it's the cheapest name in Batch 4 (~35× PE) after an F&O-regulation-driven down year. The honest caveats: (1) FY26 PAT fell ~22% because the core is broking (largely F&O), which SEBI directly regulates — the same risk as BSE/MCX but more retail-exposed; (2) the strong Q4 (+83%) shows recovery but durability vs further F&O policy is the question; (3) sub-50% promoter (28.8%). The load-bearing question is whether diversification + client-base monetisation offset F&O-regulation drag. Cheapest + scale + diversifying; regulation-exposed core is the constraint.
