Executive Summary

Solar Industries India is the country's largest industrial-explosives company and, increasingly, a defence munitions/propellants powerhouse — explosives, ammunition, warheads, rocket propellants and the Pinaka programme. FY26 was a record: revenue ₹9,838 Cr (+30%), EBITDA ₹2,750 Cr (+35%, margin ~28%), PAT ₹1,737 Cr (+35%) (multibagg/investywise/whalesbook, FY26 results). Defence revenue surged +94% for the year to ₹2,634 Cr (Q4 +134%). The order book is ₹21,300 Cr (~₹18,000 Cr defence, much of it Pinaka-linked). Management guided FY27 revenue to ₹14,000 Cr (+42%) with defence revenue >₹4,500 Cr and stable ~28% margins. Promoter holding 73.2%; market cap ~₹1.63 lakh crore at ~137× P/E.

CORE VERDICT. Solar is arguably the highest-"assurance" USP in the batch — a near-monopoly in industrial explosives now compounding a defence-munitions franchise with a ₹21,300 Cr backlog, +94% defence growth, and a credible +42% FY27 revenue guide. The moat (regulatory + capability + Pinaka anchoring) is real and the growth is visible. The single constraint is valuation: ~137× PE / ~16.6× MCap-Sales. The load-bearing question is whether the defence supercycle + FY27 guide grow earnings into the multiple fast enough; at 137× even strong delivery may only hold the stock. Quality + visibility are top-tier; the price already capitalises a lot of it.

Section 3 / The Explosives→Defence USP — Durable Moat or Priced-In?

Variant-perception crux. Consensus: a near-monopoly compounding a defence-munitions supercycle, hence the premium. Variant question: at ~137× PE, how much of the multi-year defence ramp is already priced?

3.1 Why the USP is real

Explosives is a high-barrier, licence-gated, safety-critical industry — Solar is the scaled domestic leader with global reach. The defence pivot leverages the same chemistry/manufacturing into munitions/propellants (Pinaka), where indigenous capacity is scarce and demand is structural.

3.2 Honest scepticism — five open questions

  1. Valuation. 137× PE needs years of 30-40% growth to justify.
  2. Defence lumpiness. Order timing (Pinaka/ammunition) can be uneven.
  3. FY27 ₹14,000 Cr guide. Aggressive (+42%) — execution risk.
  4. Margin durability. ~28% EBITDA — sustainable as mix shifts?
  5. Capex/working-capital. ₹2,050 Cr FY27 capex → OCF watch.
USP VERDICT. The moat is real and the defence visibility is strong (₹18,000 Cr defence OB). The investment constraint is the multiple, not the business.
