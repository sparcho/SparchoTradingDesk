Executive Summary

ideaForge Technology is India's #1 drone/UAV maker (~50% market share, #3 globally among dual-use drone makers per DRONEII). FY26: revenue ₹286.1 Cr (+78% from ₹161 Cr); a record, profitable Q4 (revenue ₹141 Cr, PAT ₹60 Cr, 42.5% margin) — but a small full-year annual loss of ~₹17 Cr (EBITDA ₹27 Cr) on the lumpy first three quarters (suasnews/scanx, FY26). Order momentum is strong: highest-ever FY26 order inflow ~₹530 Cr + an FY27 opening order book ~₹310 Cr; it received its first US order in Q4 and is expanding into combat drones (loitering munitions, long-range strike). Market cap ~₹1,200 Cr (₹285, −45% from a 52-week high of ₹520).

CORE VERDICT. ideaForge has a genuinely strong USP — India's #1 drone maker (~50% share), a deployed fleet with 950k+ cumulative flights, strong FY26 order momentum (₹530 Cr inflow), a credible combat-drone expansion (the high-value defence vector), and a maiden US order. But the honest reality is it's a lumpy turnaround: FY26 was a small annual loss (−₹17 Cr) despite a record profitable Q4, the stock is −45% from its high, and the screen fails on profitability/valuation. The load-bearing question is whether the order book + combat-drone + civil ramp convert to sustained annual profitability. High strategic USP + strong order momentum; current loss-making + lumpiness make it a recovery/story bet, not a quality holding today. Improving (Q4 strongly profitable), but unproven on full-year profit.
