Executive Summary

Amber Enterprises is India's largest room-AC (RAC) contract manufacturer (~26-27% RAC manufacturing share — "the company behind every AC brand") now diversifying into broader EMS + railway/defence electronics. FY26: consolidated revenue ₹12,186 Cr (+22%), operating EBITDA ₹970 Cr (+22%, ~8% margin), adjusted PAT ₹338 Cr (+22%) (investywise/whalesbook/constructionworld, FY26). Two honesty flags: (1) reported FY26 profit was ₹226 Cr — below the ₹338 Cr adjusted figure, and (2) one source notes the auditor flagged unaudited data — both warrant reconciliation before relying on the profit line. Railway & Defence order book >₹2,600 Cr; EMS expansion via Shogini/Power-One/Unitronics. Market cap ₹27,377 Cr at ~81-121× P/E; promoter ~40%.

CORE VERDICT. Amber owns a real RAC-manufacturing scale USP (~26-27% share) riding India's structural AC-penetration growth, plus a credible EMS + railway/defence diversification that should lift the mix over time. But it's the weakest "assurance" profile in the batch on two counts: (1) thin ~8% margins + modest ROCE make it a thin-return business at an extreme multiple (~81-121× PE), and (2) the reported-vs-adjusted PAT gap + an auditor unaudited-data flag are genuine earnings-quality/governance items to reconcile. The load-bearing question is whether EMS + railway/defence lift margins/returns enough to justify the multiple. Strong franchise + seasonality + governance caveat — handle with the flag in mind.
