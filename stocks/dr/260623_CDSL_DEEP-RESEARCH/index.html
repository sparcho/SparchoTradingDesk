Executive Summary

CDSL is India's larger depository (by demat accounts) — an asset-light, regulated duopoly (with NSDL) earning recurring annual + transaction + corporate-action fees, with an exceptional 45.14% ROCE. FY26: standalone total income ₹1,096 Cr (+11%) but consolidated total income ₹1,239 Cr (+3%) and consolidated net profit ₹455 Cr (−14% from ₹526 Cr) (cdsl/business-standard, FY26 results); Q4 PAT ₹79.79 Cr (−20.5%) on revenue ₹262.85 Cr (+17%). EPS ₹21.82 (from ₹25.20); dividend ₹12.75. Market cap ₹25,958 Cr at ~57× P/E.

CORE VERDICT. CDSL is a high-quality "assurance from the nature of the work" name — an asset-light, regulated depository duopoly with 45% ROCE, net cash, and a structural demat-account growth moat (financialisation of India). The honest caveat: revenue is market-activity-dependent and volatile — FY26 net profit fell 14% as transaction/IPO/corporate-action fees normalised after a hot prior year (quarterly revenue has swung ~44% over seven quarters). At ~57× PE, the multiple prices a re-acceleration. The load-bearing question is whether demat-account growth + market activity rebound earnings. Excellent franchise + high returns; cyclical revenue + premium multiple are the constraints.

Section 1 / Company Overview

Central Depository Services (India) Ltd (NSE: CDSL) is a SEBI-regulated depository — electronic custody of securities, plus KYC (CVL), insurance-repository, and data services.

1.1 Revenue streams

Annuity fees (per account/issuer), transaction fees (market-activity-linked), IPO/corporate-action fees, KYC + data services. The annuity portion is sticky; the transaction/IPO portion is cyclical.

1.2 FY26 detail

Std income ₹1,096 Cr (+11%); cons income ₹1,239 Cr (+3%); cons PAT ₹455 Cr (−14%); ROCE 45.14%.

1.3 Equity structure

No identifiable single promoter (widely held; BSE was a legacy stakeholder, since diluted). FII/DII/public mix PENDING.

1.4 Recent corporate actions

FY26 results (PAT −14%); ₹12.75 dividend; continued demat-account growth.

ANCHOR FACT. A 45%-ROCE, net-cash, regulated depository duopoly with structural demat-account growth is the bull case; the offset is that FY26 net profit fell 14% on market-activity-dependent revenue volatility, at a ~57× multiple.

Section 3 / The Depository-Duopoly USP — Durable Moat or Cyclical Earnings?

Variant-perception crux. Consensus: a structural financialisation-of-India duopoly compounding demat accounts. Variant question: FY26 earnings fell 14% — how cyclical is the revenue, and is ~57× PE justified through a soft patch?

3.1 Why the USP is real

A regulated duopoly with asset-light economics + recurring fees + 45% ROCE is a rare, high-quality moat; demat-account growth is structural as Indian households financialise.

3.2 Honest scepticism — five open questions

  1. Revenue cyclicality. ~44% quarterly revenue swing — how much is annuity vs market-linked?
  2. FY26 dip. PAT −14% — base normalisation or trend?
  3. Regulated fees. SEBI pricing actions can cap monetisation.
  4. Competition. NSDL (now listed) intensity.
  5. Valuation. ~57× PE on dipping earnings.
USP VERDICT. Genuinely high-quality duopoly with exceptional returns — but the earnings are more cyclical than the "structural compounder" narrative implies; the multiple needs a rebound.
