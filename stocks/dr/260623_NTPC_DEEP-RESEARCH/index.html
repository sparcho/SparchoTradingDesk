Executive Summary

NTPC is India's largest power generator (regulated PSU). FY26: standalone PAT ₹23,162 Cr (+18%), revenue ₹1,87,385 Cr; group PAT +15%; installed capacity 89,108 MW (group 90,857 MW); coal PLF 72% (vs all-India 63%) (solarquarter, FY26). NTPC Green (89% owned) — revenue ₹2,858 Cr, PAT ₹521 Cr — is the renewable-growth optionality. GoI 51.1%; market cap ₹3,54,704 Cr at ~13.11× P/E, ~1.89× sales.

CORE VERDICT. NTPC is a cheap, defensive value utility — India's largest generator earning a predictable regulated RoE on a 89-GW fleet, with an efficient coal PLF (72%) and a renewable-growth lever in NTPC Green. The screen is favourable on price (PE ~13×, MCap/Sales 1.89×, both inside thresholds) + promoter (51%). The honest constraints: regulated-utility ROE is capped ~14-15% (below the 20% bar), and growth is modest (regulated capex-paced). The load-bearing question is the NTPC-Green renewable ramp + capacity additions lifting growth above the steady regulated base. Cheap + defensive + dividend + clean-energy optionality; capped ROE + modest growth are the trade-offs. One of the cheaper, lower-risk names in the program — a value/yield utility, not a high-growth USP.
