§1 Executive Summary

GMDC is India's second-largest lignite producer and the leading merchant seller of lignite, with the Gujarat state government holding 74% — a structural PSU governance profile. The investment case is bifurcated: the core lignite business is mature, slowly declining, and faces secular thermal-coal demand pressure; the critical-minerals + rare earth pivot is the optionality kicker that could re-rate the stock if it executes. As of Q3 FY26 (Dec-2025), net profit declined 9.89% to Rs 133 cr and net sales declined 11.37% to Rs 579 cr — confirming the lignite-decline narrative. Six new lignite blocks (~360 MT reserves) come online Q4 FY26 / Q1 FY27, which should arrest the volume decline.

Verdict line: HOLD with downside-watch. The position is small (340 sh / ~₹2.3 L) and below cost; not a thesis-breaker but the FY26 trajectory is weak. The REE/NMDC partnership is the upside lever — needs concrete commercial milestones (offtake agreement, production timeline) before re-rate is plausible. Three-quarter window to validate or de-risk.

§2.2 Lignite — the core mature business

Wikipedia + Care Ratings analysis:

  • #2 producer in India (after Neyveli Lignite); #1 merchant seller (i.e., selling on the open market rather than to a captive parent)
  • Primary mines: Panandhro, Tadkeshwar, Mata No Madh (Bhuj), Rajpardi, Bhavnagar, plus 6 new blocks in award stage
  • End-customers: thermal power (captive + state-utility), brick + ceramic industries
  • Pricing: Notice for interim allocation pricing eff. 2026-05-06; lignite is price-administered (state-set), unlike coal which has e-auction premiums
  • Volume trend: Declining naturally as old mines deplete; new block commissioning is the volume-replacement story

§2.3 Critical minerals + REE — the upside lever

GMDC has announced a partnership with NMDC for rare earth element development. Per MD Roopwant Singh:

"The collaboration with NMDC brings together complementary institutional strengths and technical expertise to explore opportunities in the rare earth sector."

This aligns with India's national priorities on critical-mineral resilience. But — and this matters — no concrete commercial offtake, production timeline, or revenue contribution has been disclosed yet. This is optionality, not earnings.

REE prices have been weak globally (China-dominated supply chain). Indian REE economics are uncertain without:

  • Concentrate-to-oxide processing capability (downstream)
  • Offtake from auto/defence end-users at premium to spot
  • Government subsidy or production-linked incentive (PLI for critical minerals not yet defined for REE)

§5 Valuation

CMP ~Rs 663 / mcap ~Rs 20,000 cr / P/E TTM ~30x (declining EPS means trailing P/E rises mechanically; forward P/E is the better metric).

Compared to peers:

  • Coal India — vastly larger; better dividend yield; trades 6-7x P/E
  • NMDC — iron-ore; cyclical; 8-10x P/E
  • Neyveli Lignite (NLC India) — lignite peer; smaller market position; ~10-12x P/E

GMDC trades at a premium to peers that's hard to defend purely on lignite economics. The premium reflects the REE optionality + Gujarat-state backing. If REE thesis fails to materialise commercially, multiple compression risk is high — peer-line P/E of ~10x would imply Rs 230-280 territory (i.e., -55-65% downside).

This is the asymmetry that matters: upside requires REE commercial proof; downside is mean-reversion to peer multiples.
