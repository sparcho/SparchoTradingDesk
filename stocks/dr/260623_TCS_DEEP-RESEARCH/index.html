Executive Summary

TCS is India's largest IT-services company. FY26: revenue $30,017M (−0.5% USD, −2.4% CC), PAT ₹52,820 Cr (+8.8%); operating margin 25% (4-year high), net margin 19.8% (4-year high) (tcs/icicidirect, FY26). TCV $40.7bn (among highest ever), 5 mega-deals; annualised AI revenue >$2.3bn. Tata 71.8%; market cap ₹7,69,857 Cr at ~15.64× P/E — cheap.

CORE VERDICT. TCS is a best-in-class compounder at a cheap multiple — India's #1 IT-services firm with 25% operating margins (4-year high), ~40%+ ROCE, net cash, huge cash returns, and the strongest fundamental screen among the large-cap IT (6 of 8 PASS), at ~15.6× PE. The honest constraint: FY26 **revenue declined (−2.4% CC)** on a global discretionary-IT-spend air-pocket — i.e. it's cheap because growth stalled. The offsets to that: a record TCV ($40.7bn, 5 mega-deals) + a scaling AI franchise (>$2.3bn) underwrite an eventual demand recovery, while margins held at a 4-year high. The load-bearing question is when IT-demand re-accelerates revenue (the TCV suggests it's coming). Highest-quality + cheapest large-cap IT; the demand-led revenue dip is the (cyclical) catch. A quality-value IT name in a demand trough.
