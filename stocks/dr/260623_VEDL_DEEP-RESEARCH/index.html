Executive Summary

Vedanta Limited is a diversified natural-resources major — zinc/silver (via Hindustan Zinc), aluminium, oil & gas, iron & steel, copper, power. FY26 was record: revenue ₹1,74,075 Cr (+15%), EBITDA ₹55,976 Cr (+28%, ~32% margin), PAT ₹25,096 Cr (+22%); Q4 PAT ₹9,352 Cr (+89%), Q4 EBITDA margin 44% (vedantalimited/businesstoday, FY26). Net debt ₹53,254 Cr, net debt/EBITDA 0.95× (best in 14 quarters); FY26 dividend ₹34/sh (high yield); TSR +48.6%. A demerger (effective May 1, 2026) splits Vedanta into 5 independent entities (Aluminium, Zinc, Oil & Gas, Iron & Steel, Copper) — the value-unlock catalyst. The stock is cheap (~7-8× PE); promoter (Anil Agarwal / Vedanta Resources) ~56% with a pledge.

CORE VERDICT. Vedanta is the cheap + high-dividend + special-situation (demerger) value name in the batch — a diversified resources major with 32% EBITDA, a record FY26, improving VEDL-level leverage (0.95×), and a sum-of-parts value-unlock from the May-2026 demerger into 5 listed entities. It is also the holding-co for the high-quality Hindustan Zinc. The honest caveats — the classic Vedanta overhang: (1) the parent (Vedanta Resources) carries significant leverage, which drives Vedanta Ltd's very large dividend extraction + a promoter pledge; (2) demerger-execution complexity + how value/debt gets allocated across the 5 entities; (3) commodity-cyclicality. The load-bearing question is whether the demerger unlocks value cleanly while the parent's leverage/pledge is managed. Cheap + high-yield + demerger catalyst; promoter-pledge/parent-leverage is the defining risk.
