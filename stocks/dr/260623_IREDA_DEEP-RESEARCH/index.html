Executive Summary

IREDA is India's dedicated renewable-energy financier — a Navratna PSU NBFC lending to solar/wind/hydro/storage/green-hydrogen projects. FY26: PAT ₹1,873 Cr (+10% from ₹1,699 Cr), revenue ₹8,309 Cr (+23%), loan book ₹93,069 Cr (+22%); sanctions +39% (₹51,883 Cr), disbursements +16% (₹34,946 Cr) (saurenergy/solarquarter, FY26). NIM 3.74% (+41 bps), CRAR 20.59%, net worth +34% to ₹13,781 Cr — but GNPA rose to 3.49% / NNPA 1.29%, and Q4 profit declined. GoI holds 71.76% (Navratna; raised ₹2,006 Cr via QIP). Market cap ₹35,719 Cr at ~19× P/E (~2.6× P/B) — the cheapest valuation in the batch.

CORE VERDICT. IREDA is the "financier leg" of the renewables value chain — a policy-backed, Navratna PSU green-lender riding India's renewable-capex supercycle, growing its book +22% and, notably, the cheapest valuation in the batch (PE ~19×, P/B ~2.6×) on a ~14-15% ROE. The honest caveats: (1) GNPA rose to 3.49% (from lower) and Q4 profit dipped — asset quality is the watch as the book seasons through a fast-growth phase; (2) ROE (~14-15%) is below the 20% bar; (3) it's a leveraged lender (rate/funding-cost-sensitive). The load-bearing question is whether book growth + falling cost-of-borrowing offset the GNPA creep. Cheapest + policy-backed growth; asset-quality is the genuine risk.

Section 3 / The Green-Financier USP — Policy Tailwind vs Asset-Quality Creep

Variant-perception crux. Consensus: a policy-backed green-lender compounding its book on India's renewable supercycle. Variant question: GNPA rose to 3.49% + Q4 profit dipped — is asset quality deteriorating as the book seasons?

3.1 Why the USP is real

A dedicated, govt-backed renewable financier with privileged funding access + sector expertise + a structural renewable-capex tailwind = a durable lending franchise; falling cost-of-borrowing (7.07%) aids NIM.

3.2 Honest scepticism — five open questions

  1. GNPA. Rose to 3.49% — seasoning, or genuine stress?
  2. Q4 dip. Why did Q4 profit decline?
  3. ROE. ~14-15% — below the 20% bar; does it improve?
  4. Sector concentration. All-renewable book — correlated risk.
  5. Rate/funding. Cost-of-borrowing trajectory.
USP VERDICT. A genuine policy-backed growth lender at a cheap multiple — the asset-quality creep (GNPA 3.49%) is the real question, not the franchise or the growth.
