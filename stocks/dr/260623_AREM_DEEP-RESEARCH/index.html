Executive Summary

Amara Raja Energy & Mobility is India's #2 battery maker (Amaron lead-acid; #1 is Exide) pivoting into Li-ion cells via its Giga Corridor. FY26: revenue ₹13,549 Cr (+9.2% from ₹12,405 Cr), PAT ₹1,206.89 Cr (−5.21% from ₹1,273.17 Cr) — a profit dip on lead-acid margin pressure; Q4 recovered (revenue ₹3,460 Cr +16%, PBT ₹433 Cr vs ₹224 Cr) (saurenergy/pv-magazine, FY26). "New energy" revenue +60% (small base). Giga Corridor: R&D + qualification plant commissioning; first 2 GWh cell manufacturing on track for June 2027. Market cap ₹16,032 Cr at ~13× P/E / ~1.18× sales — among the cheapest in the program. Promoter (Galla family) ~28-30%.

CORE VERDICT. AREM is the direct comparator to the operator's held EXIDEIND — the same #2-vs-#1 lead-acid duopoly executing the same Li-ion pivot. It is genuinely cheap (PE ~13×, MCap/Sales ~1.18×) with a profitable lead-acid cash engine. The honest caveats: (1) FY26 PAT fell −5.2% on lead-acid margin pressure (Q4 recovered); (2) slow top-line growth (+9%); (3) the Li-ion cell story is distant — first 2 GWh only by June 2027 (optionality, not earnings); (4) sub-50% promoter (~28-30%). The load-bearing question is whether the lead-acid core stabilises margins while the Giga Corridor de-risks toward 2027. Cheap + real cash engine + battery optionality; slow growth + margin dip + distant Li-ion are the constraints. A useful cross-read against the held EXIDE thesis.
