Executive Summary

Blue Star is a diversified HVAC company — room AC + commercial AC + electro-mechanical projects (EMP). FY26: consolidated net profit ₹527 Cr (−10.8%), revenue ₹12,402 Cr (+3.6%); EMP + Commercial-AC +12.8% to ₹6,763 Cr (strong), while Unitary Products (room AC) was weak (₹5,332 Cr) on an early monsoon + mild summer; Q4 PAT ₹227 Cr (+17.1%) (investywise/business-standard, FY26). Order book ₹6,923 Cr (+10.5%). Market cap ₹33,908 Cr at ~52× P/E, ~2.7× sales; promoter ~38%.

CORE VERDICT. Blue Star is a better-diversified HVAC play than pure-AC peers — its EMP/commercial-AC projects leg (+12.8%, ₹6,923 Cr order book) cushioned a weak room-AC season, so FY26 PAT fell only −10.8% (vs Voltas −56%). The diversification + B2B order-book are the genuine USP. The honest constraints: (1) FY26 was still a down year (PAT −10.8%) on the weather-hit AC leg; (2) thin HVAC margins (~5-6%); (3) a premium ~52× PE; (4) sub-50% promoter (~38%). The load-bearing question is room-AC margin/volume recovery (a normal summer) + continued EMP order conversion. Diversification cushion + order book; thin margins + weather-AC exposure + premium PE are the trade-offs. The more-resilient HVAC name (vs Voltas) — but still premium + a soft FY26.
