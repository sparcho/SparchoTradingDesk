Executive Summary

Hindustan Copper is India's only integrated copper-mining PSU — a scarce, strategic asset in a copper-deficit, electrifying economy. FY26: revenue ₹3,077.92 Cr (+49% from ₹2,070.96 Cr) (highest ever); ore production 3.67 MT (+6%), MIC 27,421 t (+9%); GoI 66.14% (zero pledge); dividend ₹1.86.

⚠ PAT-figure reconciliation flag (V-26): sources disagree materially on FY26 PAT — psuwatch/prokerala cite ₹920.67 Cr (+97%); one source (psuconnect) cites ₹207 Cr with a ₹95.75 Cr exceptional; the ~83× PE on a ₹56,271 Cr mcap implies ~₹678 Cr. These are >5% apart — PAT is uncertain pending the audited filing; do NOT rely on a single figure. (Revenue ₹3,078 Cr +49% is consistent across sources.) The working assumption below uses ₹920.67 Cr (+97%, most-cited) but the discrepancy is flagged.
CORE VERDICT. Hindustan Copper has a genuinely scarce USP — India's only integrated copper miner, leveraged to a structural copper-deficit + electrification/EV/grid demand. FY26 revenue +49% is strong. But it is among the most expensive names in the program (PE ~83×, MCap/Sales ~18×) after a +150% one-year run — "priced for perfection" — and the FY26 PAT figure is genuinely disputed across sources (see flag). The load-bearing question is whether mine-expansion volume + copper prices justify the extreme multiple. Scarce strategic USP; extreme valuation + a data-reconciliation flag are the constraints.
