Executive Summary

Biocon is a biosimilars/biologics + generics company. FY26: revenue ₹16,927 Cr (+13% adj) / total income ₹17,270 Cr; PAT (pre-exceptional) ₹436 Cr (+323% off a low base); EBITDA ₹3,798 Cr (~22% margin); Q4 PAT ₹179 Cr (+64%) (biocon/business-standard, FY26). Biosimilars (58.5% of sales) rose 9.4% with EBITDA +44%. Debt restructuring cuts ~₹300 Cr/yr of interest from FY27; Biocon Biologics integrated as a wholly-owned subsidiary. Market cap ~₹40,000-44,314 Cr at ~100× P/E (on the low base).

CORE VERDICT. Biocon is a biosimilar-scale + de-leveraging turnaround — FY26 PAT surged +323% (off a low base) on biosimilar operating leverage (EBITDA +44%) + ~22% margins, and the debt restructuring + Biocon Biologics integration simplify the structure + cut interest (~₹300 Cr/yr from FY27). The biosimilars franchise (global, high-barrier) is the genuine USP. The honest constraints: (1) the ~100× PE is a function of a low earnings base (not a quality premium — earnings are recovering from depressed levels); (2) the group has carried high legacy debt (now restructuring); (3) ROE is low (turnaround base). The load-bearing question is whether biosimilar scale + lower interest drive a sustained earnings ramp. Biosimilar turnaround + de-leveraging; high-PE-on-low-base + legacy debt are the constraints. A biosimilar-leverage recovery bet.
