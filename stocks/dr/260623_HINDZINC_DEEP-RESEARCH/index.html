Executive Summary

Hindustan Zinc is the world's #2 zinc producer and India's largest silver producer — a near-monopoly domestic zinc-lead-silver miner with world-class 54% EBITDA margins. FY26 was a record: revenue ₹40,844 Cr (+19.8%), PAT ₹13,832 Cr (+33.6%); Q4 PAT ₹5,033 Cr (+67.6%), Q4 EBITDA margin 57% (udaipurtimes/univest, FY26). Silver production 627 tonnes; mined metal 1,114 kt + refined 1,048 kt (records). It pays a high dividend (₹11/sh interim FY27). Market cap ₹2,51,195 Cr at ~17.4× P/E (cheap for the quality; P/B ~53× is a depleted-equity quirk, not meaningful). Vedanta Ltd owns 60.71%.

CORE VERDICT. Hindustan Zinc is the highest-quality-at-a-reasonable-price metals name in the batch — a near-monopoly zinc-lead-silver miner with world-class 54% EBITDA margins, a high dividend, and a sub-20× PE (17.4×) that is genuinely cheap for that quality. Crucially for the operator: it is India's largest silver producer (627t) — a listed-equity proxy for silver leverage that ties to the silver-overweight thesis. The honest caveats: (1) earnings are metal-price-cyclical (zinc/silver/lead); (2) the Vedanta parent overhang (parent leverage, large dividend extraction depleting HZ's equity → the P/B 53× quirk, RPT/pledge perception); (3) MCap/Sales ~6× is full. The load-bearing question is the metal-price cycle (esp. silver) + how much cash the parent continues to extract. High-quality, silver-levered, cheap-PE; cyclicality + Vedanta-parent are the constraints.

Section 3 / The Zinc-Silver USP — World-Class Miner or Cyclical + Parent-Captured?

Variant-perception crux. Consensus: a world-class, cheap, high-dividend zinc-silver miner. Variant question: how much do metal-cyclicality + Vedanta cash extraction cap the equity value?

3.1 Why the USP is real

Near-monopoly domestic zinc-lead + a large, growing silver by-product + first-quartile cost position = world-class 54% margins; silver gives commodity-upside optionality (operator-relevant).

3.2 Honest scepticism — five open questions

  1. Metal cycle. Zinc/silver/lead prices drive earnings — cyclical.
  2. Vedanta extraction. Large dividends deplete equity + fund parent — how sustainable/aligned?
  3. Silver leverage. Silver upside is real but a minority of revenue (zinc-led).
  4. Parent leverage/pledge. Vedanta-group overhang.
  5. Valuation. PE ~17× cheap, but MCap/Sales ~6× full.
USP VERDICT. A genuinely world-class, silver-levered miner at a cheap PE — the constraints are the metal cycle + the Vedanta-parent capture, not the asset quality.
