Executive Summary

Paradeep Phosphates is an NPK/phosphate (DAP) fertilizer maker (Zuari Agro + OCP Morocco promoted). FY26: revenue ₹21,826 Cr (+29%), EBITDA ₹2,259 Cr (+33%, ~10.4% margin), PBT ₹1,328 Cr (+46%), PAT ₹1,000 Cr (+52%); production +8% (36.66 LMT), sales +10% (42.10 LMT), NPK +22% (agrospectrum/scanx, FY26). Market cap ₹14,142 Cr at ~14.17× P/E, ~0.65× MCap/Sales — cheap; promoter 57.8%; ₹1.50 dividend.

CORE VERDICT. Paradeep is a cheap, fast-growing NPK/phosphate fertilizer — FY26 was strong (+29% rev, +52% PAT) led by value-added NPK (+22%), with the OCP (Morocco) promoter providing phosphate-rock raw-material linkage, at ~14× PE / ~0.65× sales (genuinely cheap). The honest constraints: (1) thin ~10.4% margins (subsidy/NBS-framed, raw-material-cost exposed); (2) rising interest costs (a marketsmojo flag) + subsidy working-capital; (3) phosphate-price/forex/raw-material volatility. The load-bearing question is whether NPK mix + volume sustain the strong FY26 against margin/interest pressure. Cheap + strong growth + OCP linkage; thin margin + rising interest + subsidy-frame are the trade-offs. A cheap-value + growth fertilizer (cheaper-on-sales than Chambal), with margin/interest watches.
