Executive Summary

Chambal Fertilisers is a large urea + specialty/agri-inputs maker. FY26: standalone revenue ₹20,794 Cr (+25%), PAT ₹1,950 Cr (+18%), EBITDA ₹2,679 Cr (+8%, ~12.9% margin); Q4 PAT ₹145 Cr (+46%) (freepressjournal/investywise, FY26). Market cap ₹19,460 Cr at ~10× P/E, ~0.94× MCap/Sales — genuinely cheap; ₹6/sh dividend (60% payout).

CORE VERDICT. Chambal is a cheap fertilizer value name — large urea scale + specialty/agri-input diversification, growing (+25% rev, +18% PAT), at ~10× PE / ~0.94× sales (both well inside the value thresholds). The honest constraints: (1) urea is govt-price-controlled + subsidy-dependent — revenue inflates with subsidy, margins are regulated/thin (~13% EBITDA), and working capital swings with subsidy-receivable timing; (2) the diversification (specialty/crop-protection) is still small. The load-bearing question is whether specialty + efficient urea operations sustain the cheap value + dividend, given the subsidy/regulatory frame. Cheap + diversifying + dividend; subsidy-dependence + thin margin are the trade-offs. A deep-value, subsidy-frame fertilizer play.
