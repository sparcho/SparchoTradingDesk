Executive Summary

Dixon Technologies is India's largest EMS/ODM — the manufacturing partner of choice for global mobile/electronics brands entering India under PLI. FY26: consolidated revenue ₹49,586 Cr (+28%), PAT ₹1,644 Cr (+33%), EBITDA +69% (scanx/univest, FY26 results 12-May-2026); ₹10/sh dividend (500%). But Q4 FY26 PAT fell 36% YoY to ₹256 Cr on flat-ish revenue (₹10,510 Cr, +2%) — a quarterly wobble on margin/mix. Mobile & EMS is ~90% of revenue. The stock trades at ~42.6× P/E (51% below its 10-year median ~87×) and ~1.52× sales (cheapest in Batch 3); market cap ₹75,318 Cr. Promoter holding ~32% (declining).

CORE VERDICT. Dixon is the scale champion of Indian EMS — structurally positioned as the PLI manufacturing partner, high capital efficiency (ROCE historically ~25-30%), and the cheapest MCap/Sales in the batch (~1.52×). The "assurance from the work" is the entrenched brand relationships + PLI moat. Honest caveats: (1) EMS margins are structurally thin (~3% net), so this is a volume + backward-integration story, not a margin story; (2) Q4 PAT fell 36% — quarterly profit is volatile; (3) ~43× PE is still rich for a ~3%-margin business, and promoter holding is sub-50% and declining. The load-bearing question is whether backward integration (display/components) lifts margins as volumes scale. Best scale + capital efficiency in the batch; margin + valuation are the constraints.

Section 3 / The EMS-Scale USP — Margin Upgrade or Forever-Thin?

Variant-perception crux. Consensus: the dominant PLI-led EMS scale play with backward-integration optionality. Variant question: do margins ever rise materially, or is this a perennial ~3%-net business at a premium multiple?

3.1 Why the USP is real

Scale + brand relationships + PLI create a moat (global brands need a trusted India partner). Backward integration (display/components) can lift margin per unit.

3.2 Honest scepticism — five open questions

  1. Margins. ~3% net — does integration move it to 4-5%+?
  2. Q4 wobble. −36% PAT — how volatile is quarterly profit?
  3. Customer concentration. Mobile depends on a few global brands.
  4. Valuation. ~43× PE on ~3% margin needs sustained volume + mix.
  5. Promoter trend. Declining holding.
USP VERDICT. Real scale moat; margin upgrade is the swing variable. Until integration lifts margins, this is a high-multiple, thin-margin volume compounder.
