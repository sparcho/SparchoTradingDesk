Executive Summary

Avanti Feeds is India's largest shrimp-feed maker (Thai Union JV) + a processed-shrimp exporter. FY26: consolidated revenue ₹6,066 Cr, PAT ₹657 Cr, PBT ₹882 Cr (+20%), operating margin 12% (from 11%); ROCE 22.3%, ROE 19.09% (tradebrains/investywise, FY26); processed-shrimp sales +20% (16,976 MT; North America 68%); feed +1.7% (567,986 MT). Market cap ~₹14,600 Cr at ~17.27× P/E — cheap; net cash; highest dividend in a decade.

CORE VERDICT. Avanti Feeds is a cheap, net-cash, decent-returns aquaculture leader — India's #1 shrimp-feed maker (Thai Union JV) + a growing processed-shrimp-export arm, with ROCE 22.3% + a margin uptick (to 12%) + a record dividend, at ~17× PE. The honest constraints: (1) US-shrimp-export-tariff exposure — North America is 68% of processed-shrimp revenue, so US trade/tariff actions hit the export arm; (2) raw-material-cost exposure (soybean/fish meal/wheat) compresses feed margins; (3) thin ~12% operating margin + modest growth (+8.9%); (4) sub-50% promoter (~44%). The load-bearing question is whether feed-volume + processing growth + benign raw-material/tariff sustain the decent returns. Cheap + net cash + ROCE 22% + dividend; US-tariff + raw-material + thin-margin are the trade-offs. A cheap, well-run aqua leader with a US-tariff overhang.
