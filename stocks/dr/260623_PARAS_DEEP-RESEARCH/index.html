Paras Defence & Space Technologies is India's standout defence optics/optronics + space-optics company — a genuinely scarce domestic capability (precision optics, electro-optic sights, space optics) alongside a defence-engineering vertical (electronics, EMP protection, heavy engineering) and a fast-growing anti-drone business. FY26: revenue ₹478 Cr (+31% from ₹365 Cr), PAT ~₹88–89 Cr (+40% from ₹63 Cr); Q4 revenue ₹171 Cr (+58%), Q4 PAT ₹34 Cr (+89%) (upstox/marketsmojo/scanx, FY26 results 13-May-2026). CORE VERDICT. Section 3 / The Scarce-Optics USP — Durable Moat or Momentum?

Variant-perception crux. Consensus: a scarce, import-substituting defence-optics + anti-drone winner on the indigenisation supercycle. Variant question: is +24%-in-5-days / +70%-in-1yr running ahead of fundamentals, even if the USP is real?

3.1 Why the USP is real

Sensitive defence + space optics are restricted from foreign primes and very hard to build domestically; Paras is the de-facto Indian optics house. Anti-drone is a structural growth area (border/asset security). ~25% margins evidence pricing power.

3.2 Honest scepticism — five open questions

  1. Valuation vs growth. 87× PE on +31% growth needs durable 30%+ for years.
  2. Anti-drone scaling. How fast does it become a material revenue line (vs headline)?
  3. Order lumpiness. Defence ordering is uneven; OB conversion timing matters.
  4. Subsidiary consolidation. Multiple subs → margin/RPT clarity needed.
  5. Momentum unwind. After +24% in 5 days, near-term mean-reversion risk is elevated.
USP VERDICT. The optics/space moat is real and scarce — the cleanest moat in the batch. The investment question is timing/price after a violent run, not whether the USP exists.
