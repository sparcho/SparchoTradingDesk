L1 — Plain-language summary

MAPMYINDIA is a 30+ year old Indian map-and-IoT company run by the Verma family. The books look clean at the top — family owns half, no pledging, no SEBI action, long track record. But the last reported quarter (Dec 2025) was genuinely bad: sales down, margins compressed, profit down 41%. The company says this is because government customers deferred deliveries and will catch up next two quarters. Meanwhile, the business has a big forward order book (₹1,770cr), a real government/defense moat because Indian law now blocks foreign entities from holding full-resolution map data, and the world's best-positioned embedded-maps business for Indian car makers. The "AI" angle is real in the sense that they build AI-enhanced mapping products (HD maps for self-driving, Drive GPT for voice nav, a new analytics arm called ClarityX), but they don't tell investors how much of their revenue is AI — so nobody can yet prove it's a growth engine from numbers. The stock is down two-thirds from its 2024 peak, so the market has already priced a lot of bad news. This study does NOT say to buy or avoid — it says: the governance passes with a couple of watches, the thesis is moderately concrete not vibes, and the chart structure is not yet healed per Sanjeev's rule ("never enter at peaks" applies symmetrically — don't enter a broken structure without a base either).

  • SVAMITVA (rural property-card program) and DILRMP (Digital India Land Records) are ongoing consumption drivers of geospatial services. Tier: Tertiary (general coverage).
