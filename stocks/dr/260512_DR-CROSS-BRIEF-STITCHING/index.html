§2 Conditional thesis interactions — the macro overlay matters

DR briefs assume base-case macro. The Warsh-steepener brief identifies how that base case can swing. Below: conditional outcomes for each thematic brief if specific macro paths play out.

Scenario A — Warsh confirmed + steepener plays (long-end up, short-end dovish)

BriefConditional impact
Defence-RailwaysMixed. Long-end up compresses Tier-2 PSU defence multiples (BDL 76x, BEL 50x) but doesn't hurt T1 execution-proven names (HBL, TARIL with delivered earnings). Net: Tier 1 outperforms Tier 2.
Energy v2Negative for high-PE renewables (WAAREEENER, ADANIGREEN — DCF-sensitive). Neutral for PSU utilities (POWERGRID, NTPC) with yield support.
DC v2Negative for high-PE L4/L5 (AI services + apps) via discount-rate pressure. Positive for L3 physical infra (POLYCAB, BLUESTAR, ANANTRAJ) where near-term cashflows dominate.
AI PlayersGenerally negative. Engine-dependent API consumers compress more than engine-independent IP plays. TANLA / KPIT / MAPMYINDIA hold up better than NAUKRI / NEWGEN.
Silver (cross-asset, not its own brief but linked)Negative. Long-end nominal up + AI-deflation thesis = higher real yields = silver headwind. F98 redeployment buckets should de-emphasise silver dip-buy bucket if steepener plays.
DPI / India StackMixed. PAYTM / PB FINTECH face NBFC-style scrutiny on funding costs. CDSL / ANGELONE benefit from broader market activity.

Scenario B — Warsh confirmed but NO steepener (long-end stable)

  • Steepener-conditional bearish channels (silver, high-PE growth) don't fire
  • Defence-Railways Tier 2 multiples hold
  • Energy v2 renewables hold

Scenario C — Hormuz full breakdown (regime flips RED)

  • Defence-Railways: strong tailwind — supply-chain redundancy demand, AMCA urgency
  • Energy v2: strong tailwind for thermal/PSU (NTPC), headwind for renewables (cost-of-capital up)
  • DC v2: negative — global capex friction, India's comparative advantage narrows if dollar strength returns
  • AI Players: negative (risk-off)
  • Silver (linked): strong tailwind — F98 trim ladder fires faster, redeployment cash bucket gets bigger

Implication

The defence-railways book (TARIL + HBL) is the only allocation that's positive in BOTH Scenario A (Tier 1 outperforms) AND Scenario C (geopolitical tailwind). It's net-conditional-resilient. This is why operator's instinct to concentrate there is structurally sound — though F89/F95 cluster-discipline concerns remain valid on size.
