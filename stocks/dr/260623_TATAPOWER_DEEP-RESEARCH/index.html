Executive Summary

Tata Power is India's integrated private utility. FY26: PAT ₹5,118 Cr (+7%, all-time high), revenue ₹63,681 Cr, EBITDA ₹16,090 Cr (+11%); renewables (TPREL) PAT +59% to ₹1,994 Cr; T&D PAT +49% to ₹2,978 Cr; Odisha discoms PAT +84% to ₹809 Cr (pv-magazine/tatapower, FY26). Capacity 16.7 GW; net debt/equity 1.18× (from 1.05×); dividend ₹2.50. Market cap ~₹1,21,000 Cr at ~24× P/E, ~1.9× sales.

CORE VERDICT. Tata Power is the diversified-growth utility — gen + T&D + renewables + solar-manufacturing + EV-charging + Odisha discoms, with the high-growth legs (renewables +59%, T&D +49%, Odisha +84%) offsetting flatter thermal. Unlike the regulated PSUs (NTPC/POWERGRID), it offers private-utility growth + Tata brand. The honest constraints: (1) rising leverage (net debt/equity 1.18×) on the growth capex; (2) a premium-to-PSU multiple (~24× PE); (3) sub-50% promoter (~47%). The load-bearing question is whether the renewables/T&D/Odisha growth compounds while leverage stays managed. Diversified growth + Tata + renewables momentum; leverage + premium valuation are the trade-offs. The growth alternative to the cheap regulated PSUs — at a higher multiple + more debt.
